# 🎬 How to Edit Videos ## 📖 Introduction There's no single "right" editor - the best one is the one that fits your machine, your budget, and how your brain works. This is a rundown of the editors I actually recommend, starting with my own unconventional choice and working up to the heavy professional suites. My short version: **I edit in Blender.** If that sounds strange, it is - but it works for me. For most people, though, I'd point at **Shotcut** first. > ℹ️ **New to editing?** Don't overthink the tool. Every editor here does the same core job: trim clips, arrange them on a timeline, layer audio, and export. Pick one, learn its timeline, and the skills carry over to the rest. ## ☕ My Pick: Blender VSE I primarily use Blender's **Video Sequence Editor (VSE)** as my video editor. It's the same Blender used for 3D - the VSE is a full non-linear editor hiding inside it. **Why I use it:** I already live in Blender, so editing in the same program means one less tool to learn and one less thing to buy. It's free, it's powerful, and it handles cuts, transitions, audio, and effects strips just fine for what I make. **The catch:** this only pays off if you're **already comfortable in Blender.** The interface assumes you know how Blender works - its shortcuts, its editor layout, its quirks. If you're not there yet, this is the wrong place to start, and that's fine. > ⚠️ **Recommendation:** Don't learn Blender *just* to edit video - use Shotcut instead. Learn the VSE only if Blender is already in your toolkit. If you want to get into Blender itself, see my [Blender Resources](blender_resources.html). *(I'll write up my full VSE workflow - setup, strips, cuts, audio, and render settings - in a later pass. For now, this is the pitch.)* ## ✂️ The Easy Recommendation: Shotcut For almost everyone, **[Shotcut](https://www.shotcut.org)** is where I'd start. - **Free and open source** - no subscription, no watermark, no account - **Lightweight** - runs comfortably on modest hardware, unlike the pro suites below - **Intuitive** - the timeline and controls are easy to pick up - **Cross-platform** - Windows, macOS, and Linux It does everything a normal edit needs without burying you in features you'll never touch. If you're not sure what to use, use this. **[shotcut.org](https://www.shotcut.org)** ## 🏢 The Professional Options These are industry-grade editors. Both are **much larger and heavier** than Shotcut - big installs, higher system requirements, and steeper learning curves. Worth it if you outgrow the lighter tools, overkill if you haven't. - **DaVinci Resolve** - a professional editor famous for its color grading, with a genuinely capable free version. Powerful, but demanding on your hardware. - **Adobe Premiere Pro** - the long-standing industry standard for video editing. Subscription-based and resource-hungry, but deeply integrated with the rest of Adobe's tools. ## 🧭 Which Should You Pick? | Editor | Best for | Weight | |---|---|---| | **Shotcut** | Most people - easy, free, lightweight | 🪶 Light | | **Blender VSE** | People already fluent in Blender | ⚙️ Medium | | **DaVinci Resolve** | Color-focused / pro work, free tier available | 🏋️ Heavy | | **Adobe Premiere Pro** | Industry-standard pro workflows | 🏋️ Heavy | Start with Shotcut. Reach for a pro suite when you hit a wall Shotcut can't get you past. And only edit in Blender if Blender's already home. ::signature::
