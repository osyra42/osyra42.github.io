# Lore

A secret lab created a virus that turned humans into Chimeran hybrids. The main goal was to find this lab and the scientist behind it to shut it down, but it was extremely well hidden and we weren't very successful. More hybrids kept showing up over time while the search dragged on. We would eventually find the head scientist, but only through a bunch of illegal methods. These were the stories that got us there. From injection to revenge. Along the way, Operative Osyra was tasked with handling anomalous situations—otherworldly creatures, objects that warped reality, things that bent the mind. Her only advantage was that she wasn't actually human anymore. These entities sometimes ignored her entirely. And sometimes they responded to her specifically.

# Operation Chimera Characters

## Osyra
Appealed to nihilistic, action-first people. She was the protagonist—a soldier who didn't care about people but did her job anyway. Decisive and military-minded. She didn't speak much until it was absolutely necessary, and sometimes people got hurt or killed by the time she did. Dark, brooding, and indifferent. She saved them because it was her job, not because she wanted to.
She was Chimeran—a human hybrid created by a virus made in a lab. She was experimented on as a kid. The result was a human with wolf ears, a wolf tail, and elongated canines. She had enhanced strength, speed, balance, cunning, and instant tactical genius. The most obvious sign that she wasn't human was her red eyes. This caused problems in civilian life, so she hid her traits using color contacts, hats, dresses—anything that worked.

## Vanity
An AI-powered Android. Appealed to people who were obsessed with information. She kept records of everything Osyra did, since Osyra refused to do any paperwork. She also tracked the other operatives. She collected data by any means—moral, immoral, or otherwise. She did not acknowledge authority.
Rarely seen outside of headquarters. She understood tactical situations quickly and was ridiculously strong. She ran on a closed network, meaning she could not be hacked remotely. To interface with any digital system, she had to physically lay her hands on it like a handshake. That said, she was still highly observant about how things were connected.

## Uta
Appealed to female gang members. She owned a nightclub that Osyra often hung out at. She used to be the primary DJ there and eventually bought the place from the owner. She still DJed just because it was fun. More of a lifestyle character than a combat one. Into music and tech.

## Layla
Mousey, meek, not intimidating, but extremely smart. She was the emotional support of the team—she assisted Vanity with situations that required a human touch. Super empathetic.
She had a small, childlike frame despite being twenty. Her weak build may have been why she chose to focus on the digital world rather than physical combat. In a fight, she was mainly good at running and hiding. She won by outsmarting her enemies, not overpowering them.

## Korval
Appealed to macho men. Osyra's bodyguard and driver. He was American with Russian descent, had a thick accent, and a tough personality. He enjoyed peace and quiet and did his job in a stiff, mechanical way. He had almost a sixth sense for when things were about to go wrong, yet he was often found cowering faster than Osyra, especially when facing creatures from other worlds.

## Rosalyn
Osyra's biological grandmother. Very gentle and sweet. The emotional anchor in a story full of people who didn't do emotions well. She accepted all people the way they were and tried to understand them rather than tell them how to live. She just listened.
