# Chapter 10 - Child Happiness

## Overview
Nancy and Layla's morning routine at Nancy's house after Layla's parents' death, featuring Layla's coffee habit, their discussion about Vanity, and Layla's excitement upon learning Vanity is now a physical android.

## Key Claims, Facts, and Dates
- Morning routine begins at 6:30 a.m. with alarm clock jolting Nancy awake
- Nancy recalls previous day's events: meetings with lawyers, signing papers, weight of Layla's future, encounter with Vanity at McKay house
- Nancy finds Vanity unsettling: calm and precise speech, unnaturalness, violet eyes scanning with curiosity, not quite human nor just machine
- Nancy questions whether to report Vanity to lawyers handling McKay estate (they hadn't mentioned android)
- Nancy makes breakfast: eggs, bacon, toast, coffee - familiar routine helps ground her amidst chaotic thoughts
- Nancy's mind keeps drifting to McKay house and Vanity despite trying to focus on Layla's needs
- Nancy checks on Layla: room dark, curtains drawn, Layla curled under quilt
- Nancy wakes Layla gently, notes how small and vulnerable Layla looks
- Layla comes to kitchen: messy hair from sleep, wearing favorite oversized sweater
- Layla expresses gratitude for Nancy getting her stuff and making breakfast
- Layla reveals she drinks black coffee regularly (learned from Mom and Dad who had pot always going)
- Layla takes ice cubes from freezer, pours coffee over them to make iced coffee
- Nancy initially protests coffee for kids, then relents allowing one cup as reminder of home
- Layla giggles at Nancy's caffeine withdrawal joke, admits she might have been sleepy from withdrawal
- Nancy asks Layla if parents had secret projects, specifically about Vanity
- Layla casually confirms knowing about Vanity: "not a secret to me," been around forever
- Layla describes Vanity as "really advanced program," Dad's "legacy project," Mom said she was perfect
- Layla clarifies Vanity was software only: "just software," "super fancy AI that can talk and do stuff," "not a person," existed only on computer screen
- Layla says she used to talk to Vanity all the time when little via computer screen
- Nancy reveals Vanity is now different: has android body, looks human, more advanced and real
- Layla reacts with excitement: jumps up and down, calls it "huge," "coolest thing ever," wants to see Vanity immediately
- Nancy advises caution: Vanity is different now, complicated, advanced android, unknown reactions/capabilities
- Layla insists she understands but maintains excitement: still Vanity, just more now, can't wait to see/talk to her
- Nancy and Layla agree to go see Vanity together with safety check: leave if anything feels off
- Layla's enthusiasm surprises Nancy: usually reserved, now glowing, sadness replaced by pure excitement
- Nancy feels hope: this connection to parents' work might be exactly what Layla needs - piece of old life feeling alive again
