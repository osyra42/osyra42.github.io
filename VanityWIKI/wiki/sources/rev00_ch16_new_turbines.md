# Chapter 16 - New Turbines

## Overview
Nala receives a call from Layla while doing missionary work in Africa, providing crucial guidance about their father's safety deposit box and setting Layla on the path to finding answers about the missing chip.

## Setting

### African Village Mission
- **Location**: Unspecified African village
- **Time**: Three years after parents' death (approximately)
- **Environment**: Red dirt, ancient baobab tree, community-built structures
- **Atmosphere**: Peaceful, purposeful, communal
- **Sensory details**: Woodsmoke, groundnut stew, children's laughter, rhythmic work sounds

### Nala's Life at Mission
- **Role**: Teacher and builder ("Teacher Nala")
- **Work**: Building homes, community center, wind turbines
- **Relationship with children**: Beloved teacher, surrogate mother figure
- **Physical changes**: "Toughened skin but softened her heart"
- **Duration**: Three years of service
- **Living conditions**: Simple, rustic, disconnected from technology

## Key Events

### Opening Scene - Daily Life
- Nala building with clay bricks in hot sun
- Children bring her lunch (peanut stew)
- Teaching English letters to young students
- Peaceful, purposeful existence
- Connected to community, respected, needed

### The Call
- **Messenger**: Jacob, mission leader
- **News**: "It's your sister. From America."
- **Nala's reaction**: Bowl nearly slips, hands tremble
- **Emotional state**: Flood of memories, protective instinct activates

### Phone Conversation
- **Layla's state**: Nervous, rushing words, seeking help
- **Nala's response**: Immediately protective, falls into older sister role
- **Information shared**: 
  - Layla found invoice for special chip
  - Payment was canceled
  - Needs to find answers
- **Nala's guidance**:
  - Check safety deposit box at First National
  - Dad kept important contracts there
  - Key should be with his papers
  - Contact Mr. Henderson (family banker)
  - Remember: Henderson gave Layla lollipops ("business expenses")

### Resolution
- **Layla's response**: More confident, remembers Henderson
- **Nala's offer**: "I might be far away but I can still make some calls"
- **Emotional connection**: Sisters reunited by phone despite distance
- **Nala's realization**: Pull of home as strong as missionary calling

## Themes

- Physical separation doesn't break emotional connection
- Nala's protective instinct unchanged by distance

### Sisterhood and Protection
