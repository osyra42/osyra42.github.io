# Chapter 12 - It's Mitosis

## Overview
Osyra's attempts to rebuild social connections after the club incident, featuring her interactions with Taylor (who shares mitosis memes) and Derran (who joins Facebook).

## Key Claims, Facts, and Dates
- Timeframe: Weeks or months after the club incident where Osyra defended Yuki
- Osyra refuses to call her emotional state a breakdown, despite ongoing struggles
- Describes time blurring together due to busyness as coping mechanism
- Weight on chest has lightened, constant brain buzzing quieted to dull hum
- Osyra considers herself "functional again" which is all that matters to her
- Thrown herself back into studies: burying in textbooks/lab work to avoid thinking about how close she came to falling apart
- Taylor has been messaging Osyra every day since the club incident night
- Taylor sends random memes and asks how Osyra is doing
- Osyra finds it weird that people check in on her - not used to it
- Derran used to check in on her when she lived at home, but felt like job duty
- Taylor doesn't have to care but does, despite Osyra spending years dismissing her as shallow
- Osyra keeps responding to Taylor despite not knowing how to talk to her
- Osyra not good at small talk or casual conversations, doesn't know how to be normal
- Coping mechanism: sends Taylor pictures of things she thinks are cool with explanations
- Today's topic: plant cell undergoing mitosis - photo snapped during lab, extensively edited for clarity
- Osyra's explanation: cell in metaphase stage, chromosomes lined up, spindle fibers pulling apart for identical sets, ultimate replication, perfect symmetry
- Taylor's response: "OMG that's so cool!! It's like the cell is doing a little dance, right?? Like, twirling around and splitting into two??"
- Osyra admits Taylor's not wrong: cell moving/spinning/dividing with grace, nice perspective
- Osyra counters: more like carefully choreographed routine where every step must be perfect
- Taylor: "That's so pretty!! You're, like, a science poet or something."
- Osyra rejects label: "Hardly. It's just biology."
- Taylor persists: "No, seriously!! You make it sound so cool. I could never do what you do. Like, I'd just be staring at the slide going, 'Uh... it's green?'"
- Osyra laughs despite herself, acknowledges Taylor's talent for making mundane things entertaining
- Osyra: "It's not that hard. You just have to know what you're looking for."
- Taylor: "Yeah, but you know what you're looking for. That's the cool part. You're, like, a science wizard or something."
- Osyra finds compliments strange but not unpleasant - not used to people praising her work
- Osyra's response: "Thanks, I guess."
- Taylor: "No problem!! You're amazing, Osyra. Don't forget that."
- Osyra's internal response: chest tightening, doesn't believe she's amazing, just "me" but Taylor's words create small flicker of warmth in her isolated space
- Osyra reflects: doesn't know how to talk to normal people, be person with friends who shares things/lets people in
- Consideration: maybe doesn't need to figure it all out now, can just keep sending cell pictures and let Taylor call her science wizard
- Facebook notification interrupts thoughts: new friend request from Derran (unexpected since he avoids social media)
- Osyra suspicious: thinks it's mistake or scam, Derran not type for social media (polished silverware/perfectly pressed suits guy)
- Checks profile picture - confirms it's Derran (calm/composed expression, tailored suit, faint knowing smile)
- Calls Derran's number to verify, he answers steadily/politely
- Osyra asks directly: "Hey, Derran. Did you send a friend request to me on Facebook?"
- Derran confirms: yes, with him gone her days are dreadfully boring so he explored social media
- Wants Osyra to have honor of being his first friend if she wouldn't mind
- Osyra caught off guard: Derran bored? Exploring social media? Unexpected but oddly endearing
- Osyra responds quickly but sharply: "Of course" then admits she missed him and appreciates what he's done
- Words feel awkward/forced like pulling from neglected part of herself - not used to saying things like this
- Derran responds warmly/gentle: thanks her, says it means great deal, missed her too, house feels emptier without her
- Osyra's reaction: throat tightens, realizes house feeling emptier without her is significant
- Osyra trails off into silence: not good at vulnerable admitting caring
- Derran's patience noted: always been patient with her even when she didn't deserve it
- Osyra finally: accepts request, offers to help Derran figure out Facebook if needed
- Derran chuckles low/soothing: may take her up on offer, social media quite labyrinth but he'll manage with her as guide
- Osyra rolls eyes (no real annoyance): tells him not to get too excited, it's not that complicated
- Derran: will keep that in mind, tells her to take care and reach out if she needs anything
- Osyra: says she will but both know she probably won't, tells him to take care too
- After hanging up: stares at screen, chest feels strangely heavy
- Realization: Derran had always been constant presence in life, never really appreciated him until now
- Opens Facebook, accepts friend request, small smile at corners of mouth
- Derran's profile exactly as expected: pristine, professional, devoid of chaos in most feeds
- Cover photo: tasteful shot of estate's gardens
- Profile picture: same one from friend request
- Clicks on profile, scrolls sparse timeline: few posts about weather, single photo of library captioned "A quiet afternoon"
- Osyra reflects: "It was so... Derran."
- Hesitates, then messages: "Welcome to Facebook. Try not to get sucked into the drama."
- Derran's almost immediate response: "Thank you, my lady. I shall do my best to navigate this new frontier with grace."
- Osyra chuckles, shakes head: "Leave it to Derran to turn joining Facebook into some kind of noble endeavor."
- Sets phone down, leans back in chair, stares at ceiling
- Final reflection: Derran on Facebook, texting Taylor about mitosis, for first time in long time doesn't feel quite so alone
- Conclusion: "It wasn't much, but it was something. And for now, that was enough."
