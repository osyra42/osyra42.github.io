# Chapter 06 - A Promise

## Overview
Nala McKay's decision to join a Christian missionary trip and her subsequent call learning of her parents' death in a car accident, prompting her return home.

## Key Claims, Facts, and Dates
- Three years prior to present: Nala McKay (age 18, fresh out of high school) volunteered for Christian missionary trip to foreign country
- Mission: bring clean drinking water to remote village, teach basic medicine, share Bible Scriptures
- Nala saw mission as calling, chance to live out faith tangibly as "hands and feet in a broken world"
- Nala's parents (Alissa and Eric McKay) ran McKay Innovations tech business, expected her to join them after high school
- Alissa: brains behind software, programming genius who turned code into revolutionary solutions
- Eric: engineer who brought ideas to life with blueprints and prototypes
- Nala rejected parents' expectations: didn't want office life designing software/drafting blueprints, wanted direct impact helping people
- Nala struggled to explain her calling to parents who saw business as everything, legacy as belonging in their world
- Nancy Mitts (long-time family friend) became Nala's confidante: saw world in terms of people/relationships vs parents' logic/systems
- Nancy provided emotional support: listened without questions, offered tea and silence until Nala ready to talk
- Nancy validated Nala's feelings: hardest thing is staying true to yourself when loved ones want different for you, doesn't mean you're wrong, means you're brave
- Nancy told Nala about missionary trip from her church: 6-month commitment (extendable to year+) to remote village for clean water, medicine, Gospel
- Nancy warned it's not easy life but meaningful, might be exactly what Nala seeking
- Nala signed up same day, heart full of hope
- Parents' reaction: supportive in their own way - Alissa hugged her, said proud but showed worry; Eric gave rare smile, said "Just don't forget where you came from, Nala"
- Nala struggled with Eric's comment: didn't want to forget origins, wanted to honor them in her own way
- Present time (3 years later): Nala on cot in different village, phone shows Nancy Mitts calling (rare unless important)
- Nala answers hesitantly, Nancy identifies herself and says it's about Nala's parents
- Nancy reveals: car accident, parents didn't make it
- Nala experiences shock: can't breathe/think, world dissolves into blur
- Nancy asks if Nala still there, Nala confirms presence
- Nancy expresses sorrow, acknowledges how much parents meant to Nala
- Nala experiences vivid memories: 
  - Alissa at kitchen table with laptop, smooth black hair in loose bun, humming hymns/radio songs, asking "What do you think, bug?"
  - Eric in garage over workbench, pencil behind ear, letting Nala sit with him, handing tools, explaining work calmly
  - Eric's blueprint lesson: "See this, Nala? This is how we make the world better. One piece at a time."
- Nala reflects on parents' qualities:
  - Alissa: brilliant, sharp mind, quick laugh, could light up room, saw solutions others couldn't (higher logic), deeply caring even if didn't always show it, stayed up late when Nala sick, checked temperature, brought water
  - Eric: quiet, steady, endlessly patient, could fix anything (leaky faucet, broken toy, shattered heart), didn't say much but when he did you listened, made you feel safe, figured out ways to make things better, Nala's rock even when she didn't realize she needed one
- Nancy asks if Nala okay, Nala whispers "No, I'm not okay"
- Nala declares need to come home: needs to be there for them and for herself
- Nancy affirms it's right decision, says parents would want her there
- Nala begins making arrangements, mentally packing things
- Nancy offers to take care of things on her end, asks Nala to get here safely
- Nala promises to get home safely, thanks Nancy
- After hanging up: Nala sits with racing heart, realizes parents who raised/loved/shaped her are gone, she needs to go home
- Nala stands up (legs shaky), begins packing belongings: village/mission/life feels far away now, heart aches for people she came to love but knows where she's needed most
- While folding clothes/gathering belongings: thinks about parents' built life, shared love, legacy left behind
- Doesn't know what awaits at home but knows she needs to be there for them and herself
- Nala takes deep breath, whispers prayer: "God, give me the strength to do this. For my parents. For me."
- Begins long journey home
