# Chapter 08 - Somehow Good News

## Overview
Layla McKay wakes up at Nancy's house after her parents' death, goes through legal meetings regarding her inheritance, and expresses her desire to retrieve personal items from her childhood home.

## Key Claims, Facts, and Dates
- Layla wakes up at Nancy's house in unfamiliar room with soft yellow walls, flowered quilt, bookshelf with old novels/knickknacks
- Remembers this is Nancy's house, not her own - her house is gone after parents' accident
- Expresses strong desire to go back to McKay house: wants to sit at kitchen table watching Mom code/Dad explain projects, dig in sandbox, hear Mom call her "bug"
- Wakes to sound of static/faint music from hallway, Nancy making pancakes and humming to old song on boxy radio
- Nancy asks about sleep, Layla nods despite having same dream again (standing in sandbox calling for Mom/Dad who never come)
- Nancy pours orange juice, mentions big day ahead with meetings about parents' things
- Layla asks where they're going, Nancy says "just a few meetings" for people to talk about parents' things
- Layla asks if they can go to McKay house after meetings, Nancy agrees after brief hesitation
- First stop: downtown building with glass walls and echoing lobby
- Man in suit greets them, says "This must be Layla," expresses sympathy for loss
- Layla looks down at shoes, hates when people say that
- Meeting room with long table, paperwork, smells like coffee and old books
- Discussion involves assets, trust fund, company shares - Layla doesn't understand why they keep talking about her
- Man slides paper toward Nancy: "This is the final document. Once you sign here, everything will be transferred to Layla's name."
- Nancy signs without hesitation, Layla curious but doesn't ask what she signed
- Meeting ends with man saying something about "taking care of everything"
- During walk back to car, Layla works up courage to ask what meeting was about
- Nancy hesitates, then says it's "just some grown-up stuff" - parents wanted to make sure Layla was taken care of, left things for her
- Nancy says she's just helping sort it all out, Layla doesn't ask more questions as grown-up stuff is confusing
- Nancy says one more stop then they're done, can go to house if Layla still wants to
- Layla leans head against window, thinks about house smelling like coffee/printer ink, sandbox still there, Mom's computer waiting
- Feels less alone for first time in days during drive
- After last meeting, Layla exhausted: tired enough to barely keep eyes open, head heavy, legs aching from sitting in big chairs
- Nancy notices, puts hand on shoulder: "You look worn out, sweetheart. Let's get you home and into bed."
- Layla agrees, thought of going to McKay house was only thing keeping her going but now even that feels like too much
- At Nancy's house: Nancy makes tea, helps Layla get ready for bed, puts on soft flannel pajamas Nancy bought
- Nancy sits on bed edge, brushes hair from Layla's face, tells her to get some rest
- Layla asks Nancy to get her laptop and some clothes from house so she doesn't have to wear someone else's stuff forever
- Nancy agrees, tells Layla to focus on sleeping
- Layla wakes to quiet house, streetlight glow through curtains, thinks she hears Mom calling from kitchen but it's just wind rattling window
- House feels too big, too empty; misses Mom typing, Dad's tools in garage, their warm/familiar laughter blending together
- Tries to remember last time seeing parents: morning of accident - Mom at kitchen table laptop open fingers flying, Dad in garage tinkering with project, Layla in sandbox building castle pretending to be engineer like him
- Wishes she'd stayed in sandbox where everything was safe/simple, wishes she'd never gone inside/heard phone ring/seen Nancy's face when told about accident
- Realizes she can't go back no matter how much she wants to
- Rolls over, buries face in pillow, cries hot silent tears: doesn't want to feel anything but chest ache too much to hold in
- Eventually tears stop, stares at ceiling mind racing: doesn't want to be here or anywhere, just wants parents back
- Realizes parents aren't coming back, doesn't know what to do
