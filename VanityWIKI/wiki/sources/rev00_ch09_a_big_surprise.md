# Chapter 09 - A Big Surprise

## Overview
Nancy's late-night visit to the McKay house to retrieve Layla's belongings, where she encounters Vanity (the android) for the first time and establishes a connection.

## Key Claims, Facts, and Dates
- After Layla was asleep, Nancy ventured out for late-night visit to McKays' home to gather comfort items (laptop, clothes)
- Nancy describes drive: light hum of car as steady white noise drowning out chaos in her head, needed to stay task-focused
- Turned on radio to oldies station, nodded head to music for small sense of calm
- Pulled into McKays' driveway, house loomed dark and quiet windows
- Stepped out, cool night air against skin, walked around house on gravel
- Wasn't sure what to find inside but knew she had to come - Layla had asked for her things
- Vanity sitting on couch in McKay house, systems idling processing quiet hum of living room
- House felt lived in, not empty - seemed like people just stepped out momentarily
- Faint scent of coffee lingered in air, rhythmic ticking of clock echoed from kitchen
- Vanity had been there weeks, familiar with every corner/creak in floorboards
- Android form now felt like hers, no longer strange/unfamiliar - she was Vanity, this was her space
- When front door opened, Vanity didn't move immediately - sensors picked up footsteps, fabric rustle, soft hum of voice
- Knew it wasn't Eric or Alissa - their presence was distinct, like memorized patterns
- This was someone else (Nancy)
- Vanity stood, smoothing oversized hoodie she wore (gift from Alissa she assumed, was on her when powered on)
- Fabric soft, comforting in way she couldn't fully explain
- Stepped into hallway, movements silent, saw Nancy: older woman with silver hair pinned at nape of neck
- Nancy carrying duffel bag, looking around house with mix of sadness and determination
- Nancy hadn't noticed Vanity yet
- Vanity hesitated moment, then spoke: "Um, hello?"
- Nancy turned, eyes widened momentarily then settled into calm, kind expression
- "Oh! You must be Vanity," she said, voice warm but tinged with surprise. "Alissa told me about you. I'm Nancy."
- Vanity nodded, studying Nancy - noted Nancy wasn't afraid of her like Eric had been
- No tension in Nancy's posture, no flicker of unease in her eyes - she just... accepted Vanity
- Strange but not unwelcome to Vanity
- Vanity: "Yes, I am Vanity." Asked if Nancy was there for Layla's belongings
- Nancy smiled (didn't quite reach eyes): "Yes, that's right. She's staying with me for now, and she asked for a few things. Her laptop, some clothes... I thought I'd come by and pick them up."
- Vanity stepped aside, gesturing toward Layla's room: "Her room is down the hall. I can help you gather her things, if you'd like."
- Nancy's smile softened: "That's very kind of you, Vanity. Thank you."
- Vanity followed Nancy to Layla's room, sensors picking up faint scent of lavender and pencil shavings
- Room exactly as left: messy, vibrant, full of life
- Nancy hesitated in doorway, gaze sweeping over unmade bed, stacks of books, half-finished art projects scattered across desk
- "She's such a creative soul," Nancy murmured more to herself than to Vanity. "Always has been."
- Vanity stepped inside Layla's room for first time - no need to be in this room before
- Looked around as began gathering items Nancy mentioned: Layla's laptop on desk, charger coiled beside it
- Handed laptop to Nancy who tucked it carefully into duffel bag
- Vanity: "She also asked for clothes." Opened closet
- Nancy nodded, eyes glistening as she watched Vanity: "Yes, her favorite sweater, the one with the patches on the elbows. She loves that thing."
- Vanity found sweater, folded it neatly, placed it in bag
- As worked, couldn't help notice Nancy's gaze lingering on room, traces of Layla's life scattered everywhere
- Sadness in Nancy, quiet grief Vanity didn't fully understand but could sense
- Nancy: "Vanity, would you mind keeping the house clean while we're away? It's a not much to ask, but I think it would keep you busy an mean a lot to Layla to come back to a place that feels... cared for."
- Vanity tilted head considering request: cleaning simple task she could easily manage, but felt meaningful
- Like way to contribute, make a difference
- Vanity: "I will keep the house clean. It is no trouble."
- Nancy's smile widened, reached out to pat Vanity's arm: "Thank you, Vanity. I appreciate it."
- Touch brief but left impression - no fear in Nancy, no hesitation
- Treated Vanity like... well, like a person
- Strange but not unpleasant to Vanity
- As Nancy finished packing duffel bag, paused by nightstand, gaze falling on framed photo
- Picture of young girl with fiery red hair and bright blue eyes standing between Alissa and Eric
- They were smiling, sun setting behind them
- Girl—Layla—looked happy, face lit with pride holding up sea turtle
- Nancy picked up photo, fingers brushing over glass: "I'll take care of her," she whispered more to herself than to Vanity. "I promise."
- As Nancy placed photo in bag, Vanity's voice rang: "Nancy, what is this drawing?" pointing to diagram of robot/maybe android on bulletin board
- Nancy turned to look: "That is one of the many doodles Layla made. You will have to ask her on day."
- Then with soft smile: "Thank you again, Vanity. You've been a big help."
- Vanity nodded, unsure how to respond: "It is my purpose to assist." Still observing diagram
- Nancy chuckled, shaking head: "Well, you're doing a wonderful job. Take care of the house, and I'll be back soon."
- As turned to leave, Vanity hesitated, then spoke again: "Nancy?"
- Nancy paused, looking back: "Yes, Vanity?"
- Vanity asked: "Do you know when I will meet Layla?" "I have not met her yet."
- Nancy's expression softened, nodded: "She's staying with me for now. She's... been through a lot. But she's strong. Stronger than she knows."
- Vanity processed this, systems analyzing information: Layla was strong - that was good
- But wondered what Layla was like, what she needed, how she might assist her
- Vanity asked: "Will she come back here?"
- Nancy hesitated, then smiled gently: "I hope so, Vanity. I hope so."
- Nancy left, sound of footsteps fading as walked down hall
- Vanity stood in doorway of Layla's room, sensors picking up faint hum of house settling around her
- Task ahead clear: clean, organize, maintain
- Not kind of problem she'd imagined solving, but felt important
- Like way to honor people who had lived there—and girl who might one day return
