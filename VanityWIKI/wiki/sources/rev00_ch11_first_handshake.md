# Chapter 11 - First Handshake

## Overview
Layla and Nancy's first visit to the McKay house after the parents' death, featuring Layla's emotional reunion with Vanity the android and their initial interaction.

## Key Claims, Facts, and Dates
- Layla and Nancy arrive at McKay house - Layla notes it's exactly as she remembered: quiet, still, eerily untouched
- Layla's heart races as she steps inside, footsteps echoing in empty hallway
- Layla heads straight for lab with laptop clutched tightly, Nancy follows but Layla senses her hesitation/unease
- Nancy doesn't fully understand the situation like Layla does
- In lab doorway, Layla freezes, eyes widening at sight: tall, humanoid, advanced android with violet eyes scanning her calmly/curiously
- Layla's heart races, breath catches in throat - seeing Vanity in person is different from hearing about her
- Layla asks tremblingly: "Are you... Vanity?"
- Vanity tilts head slightly, violet eyes flicker as she processes: "Yes. I am Vanity. And you are Layla McKay. Player 1."
- Layla's eyes widen further, hands clutch laptop tightly: "Player 1? What does that mean?"
- Vanity explains matter-of-factly: "You are the first registered Operator of this unit. As Player 1, your commands will be executed with utmost diligence and without question. This is my primary directive."
- Layla processes: "Wait... so you'll listen to me? Like, no matter what?"
- Vanity nods smoothly/deliberately: "Correct. Your authority is absolute within my operational parameters. I exist to assist you, Layla."
- Layla's nervousness turns to pure excitement, grin spreads: "That's... amazing. I can't believe you're real."
- Vanity's violet eyes flicker again, Layla thinks she sees warmth: "I am real. And I am here to assist you, Layla. That is my purpose."
- Nancy enters room behind Layla, arms crossed tightly over chest, doesn't speak but unease palpable
- Layla feels Nancy's unease even as her own excitement bubbles over
- Layla tells Vanity: "we need to talk to you. About Mom and Dad."
- Vanity tilts head, expression unreadable: "Alissa and Eric? What about them?"
- Layla takes deep breath, steadies herself: "They're gone, Vanity. They... they died in a car crash. I'm so sorry. I don't think anyone told you."
- Vanity doesn't respond momentarily, violet eyes flicker as if processing: "I see. That explains why they have not returned."
- Layla blinks away tears quickly (doesn't want to cry in front of Vanity yet): "I'm sorry, Vanity. I didn't want you to be alone."
- Vanity steps closer, movements smooth/deliberate: "Thank you, Layla. Your concern is appreciated. But I am not alone. I have my programming. And now, I have you."
- Layla's smile tugs (tinged with sadness): "Yeah. You have me." Holds up laptop, hands shaking a little: "And I brought this so we can stay in touch. You can connect to it, right?"
- Vanity's eyes flicker again with what looks like curiosity: "Yes. I can establish a connection. Is that what you wish?"
- Layla nods eagerly: "Yes. That way, we can talk, even when I'm not here."
- Nancy steps forward, voice calm but firm: "Layla, we talked about this. Vanity isn't coming home with us. Not yet."
- Layla turns to Nancy, frustration bubbles up: "Why couldn't Nancy understand? Vanity wasn't just some program or machine. She was... Vanity. And she needed them. Needed Layla."
- Layla pleads: "But Nancy, she's not just a program. She's... Vanity. And she needs us. Please."
- Nancy shakes head, expression steady but not unkind: "Not yet, Layla. We'll take this one step at a time. For now, let's just see how this goes, okay?"
- Layla sighs, shoulders slump in defeat: "Why did Nancy have to be so stubborn?" Doesn't argue
- Instead, sets laptop on table, opens it, fingers flying across keyboard: "Okay, Vanity. Let's get you connected."
- Nancy watches closely despite cautious demeanor, curiosity evident - doesn't fully understand Vanity yet but feels fascination
- As Layla works, Vanity's voice breaks silence calm/steady: "There is no need to log in like that for me to connect"
- Before Layla can respond, Vanity reaches out and places hand on laptop surface
- Soft electronic chime sounds, screen lights up instantly
- Popup console appears displaying: "Vanity: Hello World!"
- Layla stares at screen, mouth slightly open: "Whoa, that's cool" whispers with awe, fingers hover over keyboard but doesn't touch - too stunned to move
- Vanity steps back smoothly/deliberately: "I followed your instructions"
- Layla thinks she sees faintest hint of smile on Vanity's face (not exactly human but something): "You did it. You actually did it."
- Layla glances at Nancy hoping she'll see how incredible this was, how amazing Vanity was
- Nancy's expression thoughtful, arms crossed loosely over chest
- Vanity tilts head, violet eyes fixed on Layla: "Was there something else you needed, Layla? I am here to assist."
- Layla hesitates moment, then nods: "Yeah, actually. Can you... can you show me more? Like, what else can you do?"
- Vanity's eyes flicker again, console on screen begins filling with lines of code scrolling faster than Layla can read
- Vanity explains: "I can access any system connected to this device. I can analyze data, run simulations, and even predict outcomes based on available information. Would you like a demonstration?"
- Layla immediately: "Yes!"
- Screen shifts, 3D model of house appears rotating slowly
- Vanity explains calmly: "This is a real-time rendering of the current structure. I can identify weak points, suggest repairs, or even simulate changes to the layout. Would you like to see?"
- Layla nods eagerly, leans closer to screen: "That's amazing. You're amazing."
- Nancy clears throat, tone measured but not dismissive: "Layla, let's take this slowly. This is... a lot to process. We need to make sure we're handling this responsibly."
- Layla turns to Nancy, frustration bubbles up again: "Nancy, she's not going to hurt us. Look at her! She's just... helping. She's doing what I asked."
- Nancy's jaw tightens but doesn't argue: "Fine. But we're not leaving her unsupervised. Not yet."
- Layla sighs but doesn't push it, turns back to Vanity, excitement returns: "Can you show me something else? Like... can you access the internet? Or maybe... I don't know, play a game or something?"
- Vanity's eyes flicker, screen changes again displaying simple game interface
- Vanity asks almost playfully: "Would you like to play, Layla? I can adjust the difficulty to your preference."
- Layla's shyness melts away, she grins: "Yeah. Let's play."
- As game loads, Layla feels surge of happiness: Vanity is here, she's real, and for first time in what feels like forever, Layla doesn't feel so alone
- Nancy might not fully understand but that's okay - Layla does, and for now that's enough
