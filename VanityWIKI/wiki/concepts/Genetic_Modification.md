# Genetic Modification and the Chimera Project

## Overview
Genetic modification is the central scientific concept in Operation Chimera, representing both humanity's ambition to improve itself and the ethical horrors of unchecked scientific progress. The Chimera Project specifically focuses on splicing animal DNA into human subjects to create enhanced operatives, blurring the line between human and animal, creator and creation, progress and monstrosity.

## The Chimera Project

### Official Designation
- **Project ID**: Redacted (??????)
- **Classification**: Level 3 (Confidential)
- **Oversight**: SCP Foundation, O5 Council, Ethics Committee
- **Status**: Deemed "successful" by Ethics Committee and O5 Council
- **Leader**: Dr. Osyra Renaya (initially), later Dr. Elias Voss

### Methodology
- **Approach**: Viral vector method (Osyra's thesis)
- **Process**: Animal DNA spliced into human flesh
- **Goal**: Enhanced abilities while retaining sentience
- **Delivery**: Injection (sedative + genetic material)
- **Integration**: Cells assimilate viral invader, repurpose it

### Objectives
- **Enhanced Reflexes**: Faster reaction times
- **Night Vision**: Low-light adaptation
- **Heightened Senses**: Enhanced smell, hearing, etc.
- **Physical Augmentation**: Strength, speed, endurance
- **Retained Sentience**: Higher brain function intact
- **Stability**: Long-term survival (key challenge)

## Scientific Process

### Pre-Clinical Phase
- **Research**: Viral vector development
- **Testing**: Cell cultures, animal trials
- **Optimization**: Refining delivery method
- **Breakthrough**: Cells assimilating virus (Ch. 19)

### Clinical Trials (Human Subjects)
- **Phase 1**: Class D prisoners (expendable)
  - Lie: "Experimental treatment for muscular atrophy"
  - Reality: First human testing
  - Outcome: Feline traits (slitted pupils, reflexes)
  - Survival: Hours to one month (7-Cat3 record)

- **Phase 2**: Civilian subjects (Layla)
  - Method: Kidnapping, deception
  - Series: Chimera-8
  - Outcome: First long-term survival
  - Traits: Wolf-like (implied)

- **Phase 3**: Osyra herself?
  - Implied transformation
  - Wolf traits (ears, tail, red eyes)
  - Fugue State side effect
  - Long-term survival

### Chimera Series

#### Early Attempts
- **Chimera-1 through 7-Cat3**: Class D subjects
- **Survival**: Hours to one month maximum

## Ethical Violations

### Informed Consent
- **Class D Prisoners**: Told it's "muscular atrophy treatment"
- **Layla McKay**: Kidnapped, no consent
- **Osyra**: Unclear if volunteered or forced
- **Pattern**: No genuine informed consent

### Subject Selection
- **Class D**: Expendable prisoners (terminal sentences)
- **Civilians**: Kidnapped (Layla)
- **Vulnerable**: Those who won't be missed or can't fight back
- **Pattern**: Predatory selection

### Oversight Absence
- **No Ethics Review**: "No review boards. No grant committees."
- **No Limitations**: "Just results"
- **No Accountability**: Deaths treated as "data points"
- **Pattern**: Science without ethics

### Deception
- **False Pretenses**: "Private tour" leading to surgical suite
- **Lies**: "Experimental treatment"
- **Cover-ups**: Official reports falsified
- **Pattern**: Systematic dishonesty

## Comparisons to Real Science

### Gene Therapy
- **Reality**: Used to treat genetic disorders
- **Method**: Viral vectors deliver healthy genes
- **Success**: Some treatments approved
- **Difference**: Therapeutic, not enhancement

### CRISPR
- **Reality**: Gene editing technology
- **Application**: Precise DNA modification
- **Ethics**: Heated debate on human editing
- **Similarity**: Splicing DNA, but targeted

### Animal-Human Hybrids
- **Reality**: Research on human-animal chimeras for organs
- **Limit**: Strict ethical guidelines
- **Purpose**: Medical, not enhancement
- **Difference**: No sentient hybrids created

### Performance Enhancement
- **Reality**: Steroids, gene doping in sports
- **Ethics**: Banned in competition
- **Society**: Accepts some enhancement (caffeine, surgery)
- **Line**: Where society draws boundary
