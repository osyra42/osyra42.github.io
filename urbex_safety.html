1. Dangers

1.1 STRUCTURAL HAZARDS
    The most immediate threats in any abandoned building.

    Collapse Risks:
    ├─ Floors ────────── Wood rots, concrete spalls, supports fail
    ├─ Roofs ─────────── Water damage weakens structure
    ├─ Stairs ────────── Often first to deteriorate
    └─ Walls ─────────── Can collapse without warning

    Falling Debris:
    ├─ Bricks, plaster, ceiling tiles
    ├─ Glass from broken windows
    ├─ Light fixtures and ductwork
    └─ Roofing materials

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1.2 HAZARDOUS MATERIALS
    Invisible threats that can cause long-term health damage.

    ⚠ ASBESTOS
    ├─ Found in: insulation, floor tiles, pipe wrap, ceiling tiles
    ├─ Risk: lung cancer, mesothelioma
    └─ Protection: P100/FFP3 respirator REQUIRED

    ⚠ LEAD PAINT
    ├─ Found in: pre-1978 buildings
    ├─ Risk: neurological damage
    └─ Protection: avoid disturbing, wear respirator

    ⚠ MOLD
    ├─ Found in: water-damaged areas
    ├─ Risk: respiratory problems, allergies
    └─ Protection: N95 minimum

    ⚠ CHEMICALS (Industrial Sites)
    ├─ Toxic, corrosive, flammable substances
    ├─ Risk: burns, poisoning, explosion
    └─ Protection: avoid entirely without training

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1.3 ENVIRONMENTAL HAZARDS

    Sharp Objects:
    ├─ Broken glass everywhere
    ├─ Exposed nails and screws
    ├─ Rusty metal edges
    └─ Hypodermic needles (NEVER touch)

    Wildlife:
    ├─ Rodents ──────── Hantavirus, leptospirosis
    ├─ Birds ─────────── Histoplasmosis from droppings
    ├─ Insects ─────── Wasps, spiders, ticks
    └─ Stray animals ── Unpredictable, may be rabid

    Atmospheric:
    ├─ Oxygen deficiency in confined spaces
    ├─ H2S (hydrogen sulfide) - smells like rotten eggs
    ├─ CO (carbon monoxide) - odorless, deadly
    └─ CH4 (methane) - explosion risk

    Electrical:
    └─ ASSUME ALL WIRING IS LIVE - even in "abandoned" buildings

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

1.4 HUMAN THREATS

    You may encounter:
    ├─ Squatters / unhoused individuals
    ├─ Drug users or dealers
    ├─ Criminal activity
    └─ Security guards / police

    Response:
    ├─ Stay calm, be respectful
    ├─ Do not confront or provoke
    ├─ Have exit routes planned
    └─ Leave if situation feels unsafe

2. Countermeasures

2.1 ESSENTIAL EQUIPMENT

    ★ SAFETY GEAR (Non-Negotiable)
    ┌──────────────────┬────────────────────────────────┐
    │ Item             │ Specification                  │
    ├──────────────────┼────────────────────────────────┤
    │ Helmet           │ ANSI Z89.1 / EN 397 rated      │
    │ Respirator       │ P100/FFP3 (asbestos/lead)      │
    │                  │ N95 minimum (dust/mold)        │
    │ Gloves           │ Heavy-duty work gloves         │
    │ Boots            │ Over-ankle, puncture-resistant │
    │ Eye Protection   │ ANSI Z87.1 rated               │
    │ First Aid Kit    │ Comprehensive + training       │
    └──────────────────┴────────────────────────────────┘

    ★ NAVIGATION & COMMUNICATION
    ├─ Headlamp ─────────── High-lumen LED, hands-free
    ├─ Backup lights ────── 2+ sources, fresh batteries
    ├─ Mobile phone ─────── Fully charged + power bank
    ├─ Offline maps ─────── Signal often unavailable
    └─ Two-way radios ───── For group communication

    ★ OTHER USEFUL GEAR
    ├─ Camera equipment
    ├─ Multi-tool
    ├─ Water and snacks
    ├─ Notebook and pen
    └─ Durable backpack

    ⚠ WARNING: Never use rope for climbing without proper
       training, certified equipment, and verified anchors.

2.2 RISK MITIGATION RULES

    The 10 Commandments of Urbex Safety:

    1. Research ────────── Know site history and hazards
    2. Assess ──────────── Walk perimeter before entry
    3. Assume worst ────── Every floor weak, every wire live
    4. Never alone ─────── Ideal group: 3 people
    5. Tell someone ────── Share plans and return time
    6. Wear PPE ─────────── Right gear for the hazards
    7. Test surfaces ───── Before committing weight
    8. Stay aware ──────── Constantly scan surroundings
    9. Be prepared ─────── First aid, phone, exit routes
    10. Trust gut ─────── If unsafe, LEAVE IMMEDIATELY

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

2.3 PLANNING & RECONNAISSANCE

    Before You Go:
    ├─ Research site history (archives, newspapers)
    ├─ Check property records
    ├─ Review satellite imagery
    ├─ Study street view for entry points
    └─ Check historical images for changes

    Pre-Entry Assessment:
    ├─ Structural condition from outside
    ├─ Signs of recent activity
    ├─ Security presence
    ├─ Entry/exit point viability
    └─ Weather conditions

    Route Planning:
    ├─ Primary route through site
    ├─ Multiple exit routes (ALWAYS)
    ├─ Meeting points for group
    └─ Safe havens if needed

    Weather Considerations:
    ├─ Rain ──────── Slip hazards, flooding
    ├─ Wind ──────── Can dislodge debris
    ├─ Heat ──────── Dehydration risk
    └─ Cold ──────── Hypothermia risk

    Group Protocol:
    ├─ Agree on hand signals
    ├─ Designate a leader
    ├─ Maintain visual contact
    └─ Set check-in times

3. Documentation

3.1 PHOTOGRAPHY TIPS

    Techniques:
    ├─ Use natural light for atmosphere
    ├─ Long exposure for dark interiors (tripod needed)
    ├─ Wide-angle for scale and context
    ├─ Focus on storytelling details
    └─ HDR/bracketing for high contrast

    Video:
    ├─ Good low-light camera
    ├─ Stabilization (gimbal or IBIS)
    ├─ Capture ambient sound
    └─ Plan narrative flow

    Ethics:
    ├─ DO NOT move or stage objects
    ├─ Document as found
    ├─ Use natural light primarily
    └─ Clean up after yourself

─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

3.2 RESPONSIBLE SHARING

    Protect Locations:
    ├─ NO addresses or coordinates
    ├─ Disable geotagging
    ├─ Be mindful of visual clues
    ├─ Delay posting sensitive locations
    └─ Use vague descriptions

    Why This Matters:
    ├─ Prevents vandalism and theft
    ├─ Avoids "spot burning" (security increase)
    ├─ Protects inexperienced explorers
    └─ Maintains discovery experience

                    APPENDIX A: LEGAL
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

TRESPASSING CONSEQUENCES (by severity):
├─ Civil Matter ────── Damages (UK simple trespass)
├─ Infraction ──────── Fine only
├─ Misdemeanor ─────── Arrest, fines, possible jail
└─ Felony ──────────── Critical infrastructure, intent to crime

COUNTRY VARIATIONS:
├─ UK ──────── Civil tort; criminal for railways, protected sites
├─ USA ─────── Varies by state dramatically
├─ Canada ──── Provincial (Ontario TPA, BC Trespass Act)
├─ France ──── Up to 1 year imprisonment
├─ Germany ─── Fines or up to 1 year
└─ Japan ───── Up to 3 years (Article 130)

⚠ IMPORTANT: "Abandoned" does NOT mean legal to enter.
   Property rights remain until legally relinquished.

LEGAL ALTERNATIVES:
├─ 1. Seek permission from owners
├─ 2. Join official tours
├─ 3. Photograph from public areas
├─ 4. Volunteer with preservation groups
└─ 5. Explore legal ruins on public land

                   APPENDIX B: ETHICS
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

THE CODE:
├─ Take nothing ────── No souvenirs, artifacts, scrap
├─ Break nothing ───── No vandalism, graffiti, destruction
├─ Disturb nothing ─── Document as found
├─ Respect history ─── Empathy for past occupants
└─ Safety first ────── Reckless behavior endangers all

LOCATION SHARING:
└─ Prevailing ethic: DO NOT share precise locations
   ├─ Prevents vandalism
   ├─ Avoids spot burning
   ├─ Maintains discovery
   └─ Protects inexperienced
