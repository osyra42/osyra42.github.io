# 👋 About Me Hey - glad you're here. I'm a one-person operation. No team, no studio, just me and about ten years of teaching myself things out of stubbornness - building games, writing sci-fi and fantasy, modeling and animating in 3D, cutting video, tinkering with AI, running a Minecraft server. Some of it I'm genuinely good at, some I'm still working out, and I'll tell you which is which. The usual advice is to pick one thing and master it. That's never been me. I'd rather be properly good at a dozen things than locked into a single lane - so think of this whole site as the record of that, a living catalog of what I can do. I stream on Discord, Twitch, and YouTube, with videos in between. It's more than one person probably should take on. I just like staying busy - come hang out. 🔞 One heads up: this is an adult space - open, not safe. Blunt honesty and dark humor are the culture here; malicious intent isn't. There's a real difference between the two, and as long as you know it, we'll get along fine. **✅ Do:** thick skin, dark humor, blunt honesty, unpopular opinions, giving as good as you get **🚫 Don't:** malicious intent, manufactured outrage, thin skin, daycare energy, kid gloves --- # 🧰 A Bit of Everything A running list of what I can do - tech, hands-on, creative, whatever. No filter, no affiliation; if I can do it, it goes here. **💻 Tech** - web development (HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript, Canvas), Python, PHP, SQLite, REST APIs, AI/LLM integration & prompt engineering, game development (GameMaker Studio 2), 3D modeling, rigging & animation (Blender), VTuber/VRM avatars, 3D printing, video editing & multimedia, Minecraft server administration, Git/GitHub **✍️ Creative** - sci-fi & fantasy fiction, worldbuilding, technical writing, digital storytelling, UI/UX design --- # 🎯 My Approach I do all of it myself - design, code, write, render, deploy, fix bugs at 2am. The hard part of any project was never the skill; it's starting it. The next hardest is keeping the momentum. The one after that is telling yourself you did a good job. The site is the archive - the finished, catalogued version of the work.
