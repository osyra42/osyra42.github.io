How to Become a VTuber

# 🎭 How to Become a VTuber ::toc:: ## 📖 Introduction This is how I make a VTuber avatar and get it moving, even on a weak machine. Whether you're starting from scratch or just want the tools that actually work, this walks you through designing one, customizing it, and bringing it to life. > ℹ️ **What is a VTuber?** > A VTuber (Virtual YouTuber) is a content creator who uses a computer-generated avatar, typically anime-styled, that is animated using motion capture or tracking software. The avatar moves and reacts in real-time based on the creator's movements and voice. ## 🎨 Default Method ### 🖼️ Step 1: Reference Image Before you design anything, pull together some visual references so your colors, proportions, and style stay consistent. **Where to find references:** - [Pixiv](https://www.pixiv.net/) - a popular platform for anime-style artwork - [DeviantArt](https://www.deviantart.com/) - features a wide variety of character designs - **Game or anime screenshots** - useful for replicating existing characters **Why use a reference:** - Helps maintain consistency in design - Ensures proper color matching - Guides facial and clothing details ### 🧑‍🎨 Step 2: Create in VRoid Studio VRoid Studio is a free 3D character creator built for VTuber models. It's where I start every avatar. > ℹ️ **Download & Installation:** > **Official Site:** [VRoid Studio](https://vroid.com/en/studio) > **Steam Version:** [VRoid Studio on Steam](https://store.steampowered.com/app/1486350/VRoid_Studio/) for easy updates **Key features:** - Intuitive sliders for facial and body customization - Hair, clothing, and accessory editors - Supports importing additional assets **Additional resources:** [Booth.pm](https://booth.pm/) is a marketplace for free and paid VRoid assets (hairstyles, outfits, etc.). **Exporting your model:** 1. Save as `.vroid` (editable project file) 2. Export as `.vrm` (for use in motion capture software) > 💡 **Tip:** Always keep a backup of your `.vroid` file so you can make edits later. The `.vrm` export is optimized for use in tracking software but cannot be easily edited. ### 🕺 Step 3: Motion Capture Pick a motion-tracking tool that fits your hardware and what you need it to do. **[VMagicMirror](https://malaybaku.github.io/VMagicMirror/en/)** - versatile avatar animation with or without a webcam. This is the tool I personally use and recommend. - **Best for:** Versatile avatar animation with or without a webcam - **Requirements:** None (webcam optional) - **Pros:** Custom gestures and expressions, webcam and non-webcam modes, actively maintained > 💡 **Tip:** For a $20 donation to the VMagicMirror developer, you unlock additional features including hand tracking and more. Well worth it if you plan to use the tool regularly. ## 💻 Low-End Devices If your computer chokes on 3D modeling or real-time tracking, these are the lighter options I'd reach for instead. ### 🎬 Veadotube Mini If you want to keep resource use low or just like a simpler 2D look, [Veadotube Mini](https://veadotube.com/) is a solid alternative to full 3D VTubing. - **Best for:** 2D PNGTuber-style avatars - **Features:** Minimal CPU/GPU usage, voice detection for avatar animation > ℹ️ **What is a PNGTuber?** > A PNGTuber uses static or minimally animated 2D images that react to voice input, typically switching between "talking" and "idle" states. This approach requires far fewer system resources than full 3D avatar tracking. ### ✏️ Drawing & Asset Tools If you need to draw or tweak your avatar, here are a few options across different skill levels and budgets. | Software | Price | Best For | Website | |---|---|---|---| | Clip Studio Paint | $54.00 (one-time) | Detailed illustrations and animations | [Clip Studio Paint](https://www.clipstudio.net/) | | Krita | Free (Open-Source) | Digital painting and texture work | [Krita](https://krita.org/) | | GIMP | Free (Open-Source) | Photo editing and asset adjustments | [GIMP](https://www.gimp.org/) | > 💡 **Recommendation:** If you're just starting out and don't want to spend money, Krita is an excellent choice for creating avatar artwork. It has powerful brushes and a user-friendly interface similar to professional paid software. ## 📊 Workflow Summary ### 🗺️ Creation Paths | Path | Avatar Type | Tools Needed | Hardware Requirements | |---|---|---|---| | Standard 3D | Full 3D VTuber | VRoid Studio + VMagicMirror | Webcam optional, moderate PC specs | | Low-End 2D | PNGTuber | Krita/GIMP + Veadotube Mini | Minimal specs, webcam not available | ### Step-by-Step Workflow 1. **Design Phase:** Use VRoid Studio with reference images to create your 3D model, or create 2D artwork in Krita/GIMP for a PNGTuber 2. **Export Phase:** Save your work in editable format (`.vroid` or `.psd/.kra`) and export for use (`.vrm` or `.png`) 3. **Animation Phase:** Import your avatar into your chosen tracking software - VMagicMirror for 3D animation with or without a webcam, or Veadotube Mini for 2D PNGTuber animation 4. **Streaming Setup:** Configure OBS or your streaming software to capture your avatar with a transparent background ## 🎥 Using Your Avatar on Discord Once your avatar is animated, you can use it as your camera in Discord calls through OBS Virtual Camera. ### Step 1: Download and Launch OBS Download [OBS Studio](https://obsproject.com/) if you don't already have it. Install and launch the application. ### Step 2: Add Your Avatar to the Scene 1. In OBS, click the **+** button under **Sources** 2. Select **Game Capture** 3. Set the **Mode** to "Capture specific window" 4. Choose your avatar software (VMagicMirror or Veadotube Mini) from the window list 5. Click **OK** ### Step 3: Start the Virtual Camera Click **Start Virtual Camera** in the bottom-right of OBS. This creates a virtual webcam that other applications can use. ### Step 4: Set Up Discord 1. Open **Discord** and go to **Settings** (gear icon) 2. Navigate to **Voice & Video** 3. Under **Camera**, select **OBS Virtual Camera** from the dropdown 4. Join a voice channel or call and turn on your camera - your avatar will appear instead of a regular webcam feed > 💡 **Tip:** Make sure OBS is running and the virtual camera is active before turning on your camera in Discord. ## 🖼️ Example Models Here are some VRM avatars I've built using this exact workflow - a range of styles to show what's achievable with VRoid Studio and a bit of customization. ![VTuber Model](assets/images/shop/vtubers/vtuber_4chan.jpg) ![VTuber Model](assets/images/shop/vtubers/vtuber_bigred.jpg) ![VTuber Model](assets/images/shop/vtubers/vtuber_bunny.jpg) ![VTuber Model](assets/images/shop/vtubers/vtuber_catgirl.jpg) ![VTuber Model](assets/images/shop/vtubers/vtuber_clover.jpg) ![VTuber Model](assets/images/shop/vtubers/vtuber_custom.jpg) ![VTuber Model](assets/images/shop/vtubers/vtuber_pnkpur.jpg) ## 📥 Downloadable Models Models I've made available to download and use.
