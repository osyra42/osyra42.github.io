# TEMP — Osyra: Romance Exploration

*Scratch/brainstorm file. Not canon, not merge-ready. Ideas for what a romantic partner would look like for Osyra Renaya.*

---

## The constraints that shape it

Three canon facts do most of the work here:

1. **She's not human.** Chimera-9 rewrote her body. She can't be bred by a human male — there's no biological future on that path, and she knows it. Any human relationship is, by definition, a dead end in the one way the world keeps insisting matters. That colors how she lets herself want things.
2. **She's spent her whole life being handed off.** Given up, raised by an aunt who died, foster care, *sold*. Every person who was supposed to keep her eventually let go. So intimacy isn't a comfort to her by default — it's a setup for the next abandonment. She's fiercely independent because independence is the only thing that never left.
3. **She conceals.** Contacts, hats, long sleeves. To be touched honestly, she has to be *seen* honestly — ears, tail, canines, red eyes. That undressing of the disguise is a bigger act of trust for her than anything physical.

So the real romantic question for Osyra isn't "who does she find attractive." It's "who does she let stop being a stranger." That's the hard part.

---

## Orientation

Best read: she's not oriented around a gender so much as around *safety and recognition*. If we want a label, **bisexual but deeply demisexual** fits — attraction only switches on after trust does, and trust is rationed.

- **With men** she's wary in a specific way. Most men read her size (6'4", predatory build) as a challenge or a conquest. The ones who flinch bore her; the ones who try to dominate her get nothing. The rare one who's simply *unbothered* — neither scared nor proving anything — gets her attention.
- **With women** the guard tends to come down faster, mostly because the power dynamic she's braced against her whole life isn't in the room the same way. Less to defend against, so more room to actually be curious.
- Either way, the deciding trait isn't gender. It's whether the person treats the disguise coming off as *not a big deal.*

## Partner archetypes (who could actually reach her)

### 1. The unflinching civilian (human) — the doomed-sweet one
Someone outside the Agency who never finds out what she does, only who she is. Picture a quiet, dry-humored person — a night-shift baker, a vet tech, a used-bookstore owner — who clocks the contacts and the long sleeves and simply never asks. The appeal is *normalcy*: for a few hours she's not Chimera One, she's just a tall woman who came in for coffee.

The tragedy is built in. No future, no breeding, and the constant lie of concealment. She'd end it herself before they got close enough to be hurt — "protective instinct" pointed at a lover instead of a victim. **Good for a melancholy short piece.**

### 2. The Chimeran partner — the one who needs no explanation
Another survivor of a numbered variant. Red eyes across the table; nothing to hide. This is the one where the *biological* dead-end question gets interesting:

- A fellow **Chimera-9** would, in theory, be the only one who could actually have children with her. That's enormous for someone who was told her body was an industrial accident. It might draw her *and* terrify her — the one door that's open is the one she's most afraid to walk through.
- A **different variant** (feline, avian, reptile traits) — compatible in understanding but not in reproduction. Same scars, same concealment, same red eyes, but still a dead end. Which is almost worse: so close to belonging, still walled off.

The danger with a Chimeran partner is trauma-bonding — two people loving the shared wound instead of each other. The good version is two people who finally don't have to translate themselves.

### 3. The Agency partner — Korval, the proximity option
Her bodyguard. They're already in each other's pockets; trust is operational and earned in the field, which is the only place she trusts anything. The slow-burn version writes itself: years of him standing between her and the door, never once treating her like a specimen.

The problem is it's the same shape as every dependence she resents — someone *assigned* to keep her. She'd fight the feeling hard precisely because it looks like the handler/handed-off dynamic she's spent her life escaping. Whether it's love or just the only steady presence she's ever had is exactly the tension to mine. **Best for slow-burn.**

### 4. The anomaly — the wrong, compelling one
An anomalous entity that responds to Chimerans specifically and fixates on her. Not romance so much as a dark mirror of it — being *wanted* completely, for the first time, by something that shouldn't. Horror-adjacent, not sweet. Only worth it if the story is about how starved she is to be chosen, and how that hunger could be exploited.

## My read on the core question

**Would she find a Chimera partner?** Probably the only kind that lasts. The human ones are tender and doomed; Korval is the ache she won't name. But another Chimeran is the only person she'd never have to perform for — and for someone whose whole life was performance and concealment, *not performing* is the most intimate thing there is. The Chimera-9 version specifically is the heaviest, because it puts the breeding door in front of her and dares her to want it.

She wouldn't say any of this out loud. She'd just stop hiding her eyes when they were in the room. That's her version of a confession.

## Short story — "Red Light District"

*~Chimera partner, different variant. Third-person omniscient.*

The bar didn't have a name on the outside, which was the point. Inside, the lights were kept low and red, and that too was the point — under red light, red eyes don't show. It was the only room in the city where Osyra didn't wear the contacts. She'd found it the way she found everything: by following the smell of people who were hiding the same thing she was.

He was already at the back when she came in. She knew before she saw him, because the place went quiet in the particular way a room goes quiet around a predator that the other predators respect. He had a cup of something untouched in front of him and a stillness she recognized, because it was hers. When he looked up, his eyes were the same impossible red, and slit down the middle like a cat's.

"Chimera-Four," he said, not as a question. "I can smell the wolf on you from here."

"Nine." She sat without being asked. Across the table, neither of them pretended not to look. He didn't flinch at her size. He didn't measure himself against it either. He simply registered her and went back to being still, and something in her chest she'd kept clamped for a decade loosened a quarter-turn.

They didn't talk about the labs. That was the etiquette. You didn't ask which facility, which guardian sold you, how many of your cohort came out the other side. You talked around it, in the shorthand of people who already knew the shape of the hole.

"You hide the eyes," he said eventually. "Out there."

"Contacts."

"I wear sunglasses. People think I'm an asshole." A flicker at the corner of his mouth — not quite a smile, but the place a smile would go. "Better than the alternative."

She found, to her own irritation, that she'd leaned in. Up close he smelled of nothing she had to be afraid of. No fear-sweat, no adrenaline, no one bracing to either run from her or win against her. Just a tired person who had survived the same machine and come out the other side still able to make a joke about sunglasses. It was the absence of a thing — the absence of *performance* — and it hit her harder than presence ever had.

"You're staring," he said.

"You're not running," she said.

"No," he agreed. "Why would I."

That was the whole of it, in the end. Not a confession, not a promise — neither of them was built for those, and both of them knew the math: red eyes met red eyes, but a cat and a wolf made nothing between them but company. There would be no future of the kind the world kept score by. Just this: a red room, two survivors, and the rare and ordinary relief of being a person someone looked at and did not look away from.

She slid the contacts case across the table and let him see her do it — let him watch her decide, in front of him, not to hide. He picked up his cup. Outside, the city kept insisting on its rules, on its breeding and its belonging and its lists of what counted. In here, under the red light, none of it could reach them, and for one held breath that was enough.

She'd leave first, later. She always did. But not yet.

## Aurora Pulse scenarios

*Uta's nightclub. Uta knows she's Chimeran and keeps it to herself; Osyra still wears contacts and hides her features in public, so the crowd has no idea. The room is wall-to-wall UV blacklight, bass you feel in your chest, and a layout where none of the lines are straight — walking the floor already feels a little drunk. A good place to be anonymous, and a dangerous place to be wanted.*

### Scenario 1 — She does the wanting (Osyra attracted)

Osyra rarely *looks*. Tonight she does. Somewhere on that warped dance floor is someone she can't stop tracking — and the engine doing the tracking is the predator the lab built. That's the knife in this one: her attraction routes through her hunting instinct. The heightened senses meant for finding prey are the same senses cataloguing this person — heartbeat under the bass, the specific warmth of them, the way they move. She *wants*, and the wanting feels uncomfortably like a hunt.

Candidates for who catches her:
- **Uta herself.** The one person in the building who already knows what she is and treats it like nothing. No disguise to maintain, no reveal to dread. The pull is *relief* — being known and not handled. Complicated by Uta being a fixed point in Osyra's life she can't afford to lose.
- **A stranger she can't read.** Most people Osyra clocks instantly — threat, civilian, drunk. This one she can't categorize, and the failure of the instinct is the attraction. She approaches not to flirt but almost to *investigate*, and is unsettled to find that's the same thing.
- **Another concealed Chimeran** she scents across the room — same trick, same contacts, blending into a club full of kids. Recognition before a word is spoken.

The tension: to act on it she has to let someone close enough to notice the contacts, the canines, the way she doesn't quite move human. Wanting someone *requires* lowering the disguise. For Osyra, desire and exposure are the same act, and exposure has always cost her.

### Scenario 2 — She is the wanted (others attracted to Osyra)

Osyra walks in to be invisible and is the most looked-at person in the room. 6'4", predatory build, moving through the warped floor without the wobble everyone else has — she reads as *dangerous and magnetic* before anyone knows why. Several people want her tonight, and the scenario is really about the **different kinds of wanting** and how she sorts them:

- **The confident charmer** who treats her size as a fun challenge to conquer. Bores her instantly — he's performing at her, not seeing her.
- **The nervous admirer** who can barely get a sentence out, drawn and intimidated in equal measure. She's oddly gentle with this one; it's the closest to honest.
- **The dangerous read** — someone whose attraction has teeth, who's drawn to the predator they sense and wants to test it. This is the one her instincts flag as a *threat dressed as a suitor*. Could be a thrill; could be someone who's figured out what she is and is hunting *her*.
- **Uta watching from the VIP booth** through the tinted glass — seeing the whole thing, seeing Osyra get circled, and choosing whether to intervene. A quiet third angle that folds back into Scenario 1.

The tension: being wanted is the thing she was never given freely as a kid — she was handed off, sold, used. So genuine desire aimed at her is *destabilizing*, not flattering. She doesn't know how to receive it without bracing for the catch. And the cruelest possibility: someone is attracted to exactly the predatory danger the lab engineered into her — meaning even her desirability is the program working as intended.

**Both at once:** the strongest version runs them together — she's hunting someone across the floor (Scenario 1) while being hunted herself (Scenario 2), and doesn't realize the second is happening because her whole focus is on the first. The predator, distracted by wanting, becomes prey.
