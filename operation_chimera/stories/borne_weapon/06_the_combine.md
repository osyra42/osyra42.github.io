# Chapter 6 - The Combine

Transport was a black SUV with tinted windows and a driver who didn't speak. It felt less like a ride and more like an extradition.

The coordinates led to a private airstrip where a twin-prop, unmarked and utilitarian, swallowed me and two other recruits - a wiry man who kept cracking his knuckles and a woman with a thousand-yard stare who looked at nothing at all. We flew in silence, the drone of the engines a physical pressure in my ears.

The bottle was still on my kitchen counter, two hundred miles behind me. I'd looked at it on the way out and left it there.

We landed on a dusty strip carved into dense evergreen forest. The air was thin and cold and smelled of pine resin and damp earth. A concrete bunker squatted at the tree line. A sign over the heavy double doors: **TITAN REACH - ASSESSMENT & ACCLIMATIZATION FACILITY.**

Inside was harsh fluorescence and echoing linoleum. A bored sergeant at a kiosk took my name, scanned my retina, slapped a plastic wristband on me. "Welcome to the Combine, cadet. Follow the green line. Don't wander. Don't touch anything."

The green line was a literal strip of luminous tape on the floor. It led through a maze of corridors. The place hummed - sterile, purposeful. Clatter of gear. Murmur of voices behind closed doors. The distant thump of something heavy being dropped on a mat.

And everywhere, the stares.

The first cluster saw me as I was funneled into a holding area - a gymnasium converted into a processing hub, rows of temporary cots and locker bays. Maybe two dozen people in various stages of intake, all hard-looking, all sizing each other up.

My hood was up. My tail was visible. The tips of my ears came through my hair.

The murmurs started like a rustle of dry leaves.

"...the hell is that?"
"Check out the tail. No way."
"Costume party's that way, freak."

A big guy with a shaved head and a neck like a tree stump nudged his friend, a lanky redhead. "Look, a Fido. You lose your master, puppy?"

The redhead grinned. "Maybe she's here for the K-9 unit. Wrong door, Furry."

Heat came up my neck. The old kind. The shameful kind, not the biological one.

They're different. I'd never had cause to notice before, and I noticed then, standing on a green line with a duffel: the thing in the fall that makes me want is machinery. It's chimeran. It runs on daylight and it has a courier and a dosage.

This isn't that. This is older than the serum. Nobody engineered the part of me that goes hot when a man calls me a dog - that was installed the ordinary way, by people, in rooms, over years, and there is no tablet for it and there never will be.

I kept my eyes on the green line and my face blank. *Don't react. Don't give them the satisfaction.* Same rule as every lab, every school hallway, every crowded street. I've been running that rule so long it isn't a decision anymore, it's a reflex, and the tragedy of a good reflex is you stop noticing what it costs to fire.

A staff member in grey fatigues appeared with a clipboard. "Osyra. With me. Custom fitting."

I broke from the line and felt every eye bore into my back as I followed him out a side door.

That became the rhythm of the day. Brief periods in the common areas, punctuated by constant solitary summons.

I understood the pattern by the third one and I have never decided whether it was designed. Every time the room started to get used to me, they took me out of it. Every time I came back I was new again, and a new thing in a room full of frightened people is a target. If someone drew that up on a whiteboard, it's the most elegant cruelty I've ever been on the receiving end of. If nobody did - if it's just what happens when a bureaucracy processes an exception - that's worse, because then there's nobody to be angry at.

The gear fitting was in a small armory. A taciturn technician measured me with laser calipers, brow furrowed. "Cranial circumference accounting for auricular structures… cervical spine to lumbar, note the caudal integration point… we'll need a custom helmet, modified body armor for range of motion and tail articulation." He muttered into a headset. "Yeah, send up the prototyping putty. It's the chimera candidate."

*It's the chimera candidate.* Not *she's here*. Not even a name. I was standing close enough to touch him.

Back in the gym, a new group had arrived. The whispers were louder now. More confident.

"Ugly as sin. What kind of sick mod is that?"
"Bet it smells like wet dog."

I found a vacant cot in a corner and sat with my knees pulled up, making myself small. Six foot four and making myself small. The stares had weight.

Then a different summons. "Osyra. Medical Bay 3. Vaccine protocol."

In a stark white room a doctor with a nervous smile consulted a thick digital file. "Ah, yes. Subject presents with a recombinant retro-viral integration. Standard immunoboosters are contraindicated. We'll be administering a tailored series to avoid triggering an autoimmune response against the non-human cellular lines."

*The non-human cellular lines.* Mine. He meant mine. He said it to the file.

The shots burned - a deep aching cold spreading out from the sites.

Each time I came back to the gym the hostility had crystallized a degree further. I wasn't a curiosity anymore. I was a target, and I understood exactly what for, because it's the same everywhere and I've watched it work my whole life.

Two dozen people in that room were terrified. Every one of them had signed something they didn't fully read to come to a bunker in a forest to be tested at something they might fail. That fear has to go somewhere. It cannot go up the chain - the chain is what they're afraid of. So it goes sideways, and it lands on whatever's furthest from the middle, and in that gymnasium the furthest thing from the middle was six foot four with ears.

I was doing them a service. That's the part that sits badly. Every joke about the dog was a man moving his fear off his own back and onto mine, and it worked - I could see it working, I could see the shoulders come down after - and none of them will ever know they used me for it.

"Hey, Mutant," the big one - Stumps - called as I came back from medical with my arm throbbing. "They giving you your rabies shot? Should be mandatory."

A few chuckles rippled around him.

I kept walking. Tail rigid. Ears flat inside my hood.
