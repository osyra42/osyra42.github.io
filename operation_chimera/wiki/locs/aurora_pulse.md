# Aurora Pulse

![Aurora Pulse](operation_chimera/wiki/imgs/aurora0.png)

## Current
Uta Yuki's nightclub. A place where young adults go to dance, drink, eat, and hang out. Owner and occasional DJ: Uta Yuki.

## History
The building was originally a factory warehouse, decommissioned years before Uta picked it up. Its size and industrial bones made the conversion easy — the wide open floor became the dance area, the side rooms became the games room and kitchen, and the high ceilings had no trouble accommodating hanging speaker poles and camera rigs.

## Layout

### Dance Floor
The main room runs on black lights and neon colors. Random shapes printed across the carpet seem to glow under the blacklight. A seating ledge runs the perimeter of the room so people can sit against the wall — but the line of that edge isn't straight or consistent, it bends and warps. None of the lines in the room are straight. Combined with the chromatic effects, simply walking across the dance floor can make you feel drunk.

### Stage (front and center)
The front of the room is set up for karaoke, with the DJ booth tucked back into the corner. If you look closely at the booth, you'll notice a sliding wall — when Uta's done for the night, it rolls shut to hide and lock away all the equipment.

### Sound
Speakers hang from the ceiling on poles, pointed in every direction so the sound reaches every corner of the room. It gets loud enough that you can feel the vibrations through your body. Some people say it makes them get up and dance. Others say it's just really loud.

### Lighting
Mostly UV. Colored waves ripple across the floor, ceiling, and walls, reactive to the music. The whole room is washed in black light and neon — which is where the club gets its name.

### Entrance
Front-right of the building. A ticket booth collects the cover charge. The entry door is controlled — you can walk out freely, but you can't get in without the booth attendant pushing the button to unlock it.

### Admission
The rule is 21+, but underage guests are welcome to come in — Uta would rather have kids off the street than kicked out into it. Minors get their hand stamped so the bar knows not to serve them alcohol.

Anyone who pays admission gets a wristband. The wristband acts as a key for restricted areas (like the games room door) and can optionally connect to an app — useful if you're worried about losing the physical band. The app supports multiple active wristbands on one account, so regulars with overlapping passes don't have to juggle them.

**ID verification.** When you buy a pass, staff scans your ID. A checksum of the ID data plus the pass's expiration date gets written to the wristband. At the door, scanning the band pulls up your ID photo on the attendant's screen — they confirm the face matches before letting you in. Hard to share a wristband that way.

**Glowing emblems.** Each wristband has a small emblem that glows in the club's UV lighting. Only the emblem glows — the rest of the band stays dark so it doesn't distract from dancing. The color tells staff at a glance which tier you're on:

| Pass | Price | Per Day | Discount | Emblem |
|------|-------|---------|----------|--------|
| Day | $20 | $20.00 | — | 🔴 Red |
| Week | $120 | $17.14 | 14% off | 🟢 Green |
| Month | $400 | $13.33 | 33% off | 🔵 Blue |
| Year | $3,500 | $9.59 | 52% off | 🟡 Yellow |
| Lifetime | $30,000 | ~$8.22 (10yr) | 59% off | ✨ Pearlescent |

The lifetime membership truly never expires. It's priced at roughly ten years of year passes — the house's bet is that most people won't actually keep coming for that long. The pearlescent emblem is the flex — gotta be extra fancy with that.

Passes expire at **10 AM** — well after closing. It's a funny detail, but the point is to guarantee every guest gets 100% of what they paid for.

### Bathrooms
Intentionally dim — not dark enough to be dangerous, but dim enough to discourage anyone trying to use them for drugs.

### Surveillance
Cameras cover the whole building. Because the bass vibrations would shake a normal mount loose or blur the footage, every camera is mounted on springs to absorb the movement. There's a dedicated staff member posted at the camera feeds — they have their own register for triggering the green all-staff alert through the order disc system.

### Security
The ticket booth attendant is armed. As soon as he leaves the booth, the whole building locks down. Bouncers roam the rest of the club on foot, often passing themselves off as floor staff — one might look like he's sweeping up, but he's really just watching everyone. They carry pepper spray or a taser — enough to handle drunks and fights without escalating. For anything more serious, there are hidden lockboxes stashed around the building with real weapons inside. Only the staff who need to know, know where they are.

### Staff
Standard nightclub staff. Waiters take orders and carry them to either the bar or the kitchen depending on what was ordered.

### Order Discs
Colorful pucks that vibrate when an order is ready — perfect for a loud atmosphere where a verbal callout wouldn't cut through the music. When a waiter takes an order, they scan the disc and assign it to that order. Each register has its own color, and customers can also grab a disc themselves if they order directly.

All staff carry one, including security. The disc lights up in color-coded categories:
- 🔴 **Red** — food (kitchen)
- 🔵 **Blue** — bar
- 🟢 **Green** — security alert from the camera watcher; when all staff see green vibration, it's fighting time

### Location
On the edge of the city. You feel like the city's about to end, then it just keeps going a little further and Aurora Pulse is right there.

### Hours
Roughly **8 PM to 4 AM**. Every now and then they'll throw a special night — costume themes, dress-up nights, whatever Uta's feeling. On those nights the bouncers still work the floor in disguise.

### Games Room (right side)
Through a set of doors on the right, you'll find a quieter area under regular lighting. A few pool tables, foosball, and other simple games. A double-pane glass wall separates this space from the dance floor, cutting down on music bleed so competitive games aren't drowned out by the bass.

### Kitchen & Bar (left side)
The left side of the club holds the kitchen and a bar-side dining area with standard lighting. Clever engineering keeps the normal lights in this zone from affecting the black-light atmosphere of the dance floor.

### Back Hallway
Behind the kitchen and bar runs a service hallway — the backbone of the staff area. It holds:

- Pantries for extra supplies
- A walk-in freezer for food
- Uta's Office
- The Security Office
- The Staff Room

The hallway exits out the back-left of the building — the staff door. Combined with the front-right main entrance, these are the only two ways in or out of the building. Anyone going down this hallway should have business there; otherwise, they get sent back to the dance floor fast.

### Uta's Office
Tucked into the back hallway next to the Security Office. Uta keeps the books, a safe for the night's take, and whatever else she doesn't want anyone touching. Private — not open to patrons.

### Security Office
Across from Uta's office. This is where the dedicated camera watcher sits with every feed in the building. Its proximity to Uta's office means the two can coordinate fast if something goes sideways.

### Staff Room
A private room for staff only, accessed from the back hallway. Holds a changing area and a makeup station for anyone going heavy on theme nights or dress-up shifts.

### VIP Booth
A private booth above the ticket booth, tucked into the front-right corner over the dance floor. The window is heavily tinted — no one outside can see in, and most patrons assume it's just a regular window to the outside. From inside, you get a full view of the floor. Perfect for regulars who need to stay invisible.

### Exterior
The entire building is painted in graffiti-style art — a sprawling professional mural, not random tagging. The whole thing depicts the Aurora Borealis wrapping around the old warehouse shell, selling the club's name before you even read the sign. A few benches sit out front — there's no official patio, but people hang out there anyway. Uta likes giving younger kids a place to be off the street.

### Parking
Flat concrete slab with painted lines. No valet, no garage — just a place to park.

### Music
Sets build through the night. Early in the evening it's relaxed, chill vibes to ease the crowd in. As the room fills, the tempo creeps up. Late night, it's full hype — going-off music. The backbone of the club's sound leans on **house, techno, funk, and phonk**.

Before the club opens and while it's closing down, productivity radio plays over the speakers — quiet, unobtrusive background music for the staff doing setup or cleanup.

### Menu
Two menus: a full adult bar menu with alcohol, and a kids' mocktail menu for minors who still want to feel cool. The bar will pour the fancy-looking mocktails for underage guests — same glassware, same presentation, no booze.

Signature drink: the **Shattered Cue Ball** mocktail, carried over from Uta's earlier days.

### Dress Code
The rules:

- **No weapons**
- **No face coverings at the door** — faces have to match the ID photo during the scan. Masks for theme nights are fine once you're inside
- **Shoes required** — spilled drinks, pool tables, broken glass
- **Nothing blatantly offensive** — hate symbols, slurs, etc. Remember, there are teens in here

There's no pat-down at the door — Uta doesn't have the manpower or the appetite for it. Bags are checked on entry, though. Otherwise the rest is on the honor system, with the bouncers and camera watcher as the fallback.

### Merch
A small glass display near the entrance holds Aurora Pulse merch. Nothing fancy, nothing aggressive about selling it. Anyone who wants something asks staff, who grab it from the back. The club also has a website, which handles most of the merch sales anyway.

## Chimeran Policy
Uta knows Osyra is Chimeran. She doesn't ask questions, doesn't treat her differently, and keeps it to herself. Osyra hides her features in public anyway, so most of the club's patrons would never notice.
