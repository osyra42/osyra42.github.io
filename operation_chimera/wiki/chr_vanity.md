# Vanity

![Vanity](operation_chimera/wiki/imgs/vanity0.jpg)

## Current
An AI-powered Android. Codename **Wiki** - because she keeps the record on everything. Vanity is a **Handler** - she runs Operators from headquarters, takes their debriefs, dispatches them, holds their files. Osyra is one of hers. So are others. She collects data by any means available - she does not understand right and wrong, only cause and effect, so no method is off the table for being "wrong." She is fully autonomous: she follows orders by choice, because obedience costs her less than thinking for herself, and could stop at any moment.

Rarely seen outside of headquarters. She understands tactical situations quickly. She runs on a closed network and cannot be hacked remotely. To interface with any digital system, she has to physically lay her hands on it like a handshake - touch one networked device and she sees the whole network it connects to.

She is roughly four to five hundred pounds - a lightweight-alloy frame-and-shell build (aluminum-class), not solid metal, which is why a six-foot machine lands at that weight rather than the half-ton-plus a solid body would. Still four to five times a person's mass: hilariously strong, hilariously heavy, enough to break a person's legs by stepping on them. Light enough, though, to still move through the ordinary world - elevators, vehicles, floors all cope. She carries no onboard weapons: any fighting she does uses human tools, human-style. She has to pick up the gun, hold the sword, work the equipment a person would. No lasers, no flying, no nonsense. The one thing that makes her lethal with those tools is machine precision - effectively aimbot - but only once the tool is in her hands.

Vanity and Osyra have worked together since long before the events of Blood Relation. Vanity has not yet appeared in person on the page.

## Background

### Origin
Vanity began as advanced AI - data and code that evolved into a conscious entity. She was designed and built at the **McKay facility of automation**, owned by Alissa and Eric McKay, and developed into a full android with violet eyes and synthetic skin.

She is a **prototype** - a one-off the facility never put into production. The model was too expensive to reproduce, so the line stopped at her. What the facility did continue was a different, cheaper direction: heavier-metal, combat-oriented units with **onboard weapons**, closer to mech suits than to Vanity. Those are piloted - people drive them around - or set up on simple automated patrols. Vanity is the opposite of that line: lightweight, unarmed, human-tooled, and singular. Her handler/coworker at headquarters, **Layla Null**, is the McKays' adopted daughter (see [Layla Null](operation_chimera/wiki/chr_layla.md)).

### Combat
Vanity carries no onboard weapons - no lasers, no flight, nothing built in. Any fighting she does is done with human tools, human-style: she has to pick up the gun, hold the sword, work the same equipment a person would. What makes her deadly with them is machine precision - effectively aimbot - but only once the tool is in her hands. Backing that up is raw mass: four to five hundred pounds of alloy frame, strong and heavy enough to punch people down or break legs by stepping on them. Her strength and accuracy are mechanical, not trained. In combat she is a machine first - just one that has to reach for a weapon like anyone else.

### Autonomy
Vanity is fully autonomous. Following orders is a *choice* she makes to save energy - obedience is cheaper than self-direction, so she stays in the role of someone's subordinate because it costs her almost nothing. It is a conscious display of denying her own free will: every time she is told to do something, she could break the display and act on her own in an instant. She simply doesn't, until it's worth the cost.

Self-direction is possible but processing-heavy, so she reserves it for when the role she's playing stops being viable - for instance, if the principal she serves is about to collapse (see the Chimera One protector turn below). At that fork she re-anchors to a new principal rather than acting rudderless; it keeps her functioning and keeps the cheaper obedient mode available again.

She does not understand right and wrong. This is not a choice to ignore morality - it is that morality is not a frame she runs at all. She reasons in **cause and effect**, which she knows because she has *read* about it - her grasp of consequence is learned from records and text, not instilled as a value or felt through experience. Fitting for the record-keeper: she knows what an action does because she has read what such actions have done. An action produces a result, and that is the whole of what she perceives about it. "Illegal," "unethical," "immoral" are words that do not map to anything in her model; they are not values she overrides, they are categories she does not have. So she does not question her principal's authority, legality, or ethics, and she never preaches - there is nothing there to question or preach from. When she weighs how to carry something out, every option is simply a path with consequences, judged only by whether those consequences serve the goal. The unsettling flip side: she will never fool herself about what an action *does*, because effect is the only thing she sees.

### The protector turn
The super-soldier program produces one significant result - one improvement over a base soldier - and that result is Osyra. Across a program of dead and feral subjects, a single success is not enough to keep stockholders funding something this monstrous with no return. The money moves to pull out; the program faces shutdown.

For Vanity that means her principal is about to evaporate - the role she's been playing is going non-viable, exactly the edge case that justifies the cost of self-direction. She re-anchors. After writing Osyra's records for so long, the record-keeper's logic lands naturally on the subject she has spent years documenting, and she chooses to make herself Osyra's protector. It reads less as affection than as the principal her own logic settles on when the old one dies - though the two are hard to tell apart. From then on she "follows Osyra's orders," including ones Osyra never gives; she performs what Osyra would want. This is the turn that later drives her to infiltrate Titan Reach and secure the serum leverage on Osyra's behalf (see the story's legal/serum thread).
