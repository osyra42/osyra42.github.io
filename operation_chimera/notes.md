# Operation Chimera — Notes

## Chapter 01: Blood Relation — Photo Album Scene

After Korval delivers the Vanity/Ryan news and Rosalyn brings tea, she pulls out a family photo album. Flipping through it page by page becomes the throughline that carries the conversation. Each turn of the page is a natural beat break.

### Page Order

**1. Older generations** — great-grandparents, distant relatives. Low-stakes warm-up. Rosalyn does most of the talking; Osyra listens. Establishes the rhythm of the scene.

**2. Osyra's mother** — Rosalyn explains, gently, that Osyra's parents were ridiculously poor — couldn't afford to keep her, couldn't afford much of anything. That's why she was given up. A sad, sympathetic story rather than a scandalous one. Rosalyn likely carries some guilt about not being able to step in herself. Rosalyn does the telling; Osyra reacts internally more than out loud.

**3. The aunt who raised her** — shared grief from opposite ends of her life. Rosalyn asks what her sister's final years were like; Osyra has real answers because she actually remembers her. This is the page where Osyra's guard drops a little.

**4. Hunting photos (cover-story probing)** — a late husband, grandfather, or uncle posed with a rifle and a deer. Rosalyn says something like *"He'd have liked hearing about your work — sounds a bit like what he did."* Now Osyra has to play her "combat unit, dangerous animals" cover story to someone who'd recognize a fake. The probing happens without Rosalyn meaning to probe.

**5. The close (Rosalyn's own quiet burden)** — Rosalyn shuts the album and lingers on it. Mentions she'd been hoping the agency would call for years. The house is quiet. Her hands aren't what they were. She doesn't make it a complaint — just lets it sit. Mirrors what Osyra is already not saying. Gives Osyra a reason to stay beyond the mission, which sets up the nap and the Polaroid coming back later.

### Voice Constraints

- **Rosalyn**: warm, accommodating, asks once and accepts the answer. Soft phrasing — "honey," "sweetheart." Drops topics gracefully. Doesn't push.
- **Osyra**: spoken answers stay clipped and military ("She was kind." "I don't remember." "Combat unit."). The internal monologue does the heavy lifting — sarcasm, exhaustion, awareness that the woman across from her doesn't know what's sitting in her living room.

### Flashback Device

For each *critical* family member, Osyra gets a brief flashback before she answers Rosalyn out loud. Pattern: **Rosalyn says something → private flashback in Osyra's head → clipped, military reply.** Rosalyn sees the clamped answer; the reader sees the weight behind it. Justifies why her spoken responses stay short.

Use sparingly — reserve it for members who carry real emotional charge:
- **The mother** — Osyra has no memories of her. The "flashback" is the *absence* — a beat of trying to picture her and getting nothing, or a single fragment her aunt once mentioned. The void is the point.
- **The aunt** — richest material. Specific sensory beats: smell of her kitchen, a song she hummed, the morning she didn't get up. This is where Osyra's guard cracks.
- **The hunter (page 4)** — only if we establish Osyra met him as a child. If not, skip — the *absence* of a flashback underscores that this is Rosalyn's family, not hers yet.

No flashback for the older generations on page 1. Reserving the device makes it land when it does fire.

### Tradeoff to Watch

A photo-album scene risks becoming a backstory dump. Counterweight: keep the physical pressure of fatigue going — the chair, the tea, the warmth. The album should feel like something happening *to* Osyra, not an interview she's sitting through.

### Saved for Later

A photo of Osyra herself as a baby/child — saved for after the nap, when the Polaroid question comes back around. Rosalyn saying "you have your mother's eyes" hits harder when Osyra is wearing contacts to hide red ones.
