# The Hunting Lodge

## Current
Osyra's home. A hunting lodge at the dead end of a logging road, set up for her by the Agency as cover. The story it tells is simple: a hunter lives here. A tall woman who keeps odd hours and hauls strange cargo in and out is just someone who hunts - nothing to look twice at. The disguise is the house itself, and it works because it never has to be explained.

## Why the Agency chose it
The lodge launders what Osyra actually does. Her work means coming and going at strange hours and bringing back things that shouldn't be examined too closely - containment boxes, equipment, the occasional remains of an assignment. In a hunting lodge, all of that reads as ordinary. A hunter with gun cases, axes, and game is invisible. It hides what she hunts by making her look like she only hunts animals.

## Look & feel
- **Cedar throughout** - walls gone honey-dark with age; the place smells of cedar and old smoke
- **Taxidermy of ordinary animals** - a whitetail buck over the cold fireplace, a brace of pheasants mid-flush, a bobcat with glass eyes on a shelf
- **Antler chandelier** - a dozen sheds wired together over the main room, "rustic luxury" that mostly reads as a lot of dead animals overhead
- Osyra chose none of the decor; the Agency dressed the set before she moved in. She's only added pieces a job left worth keeping.

## Equipment, hidden in plain sight
Her gear blends into the hunting theme without effort:
- The **charge rifle** stands in the door rack between a hunting bow and a never-fired lever-action
- **Lead-lined containment boxes** stack in the mudroom, passing for gun cases
- The **breaching tool** hangs on a pegboard of axes and saws and looks like family

A stranger walking through sees a hunter's home and nothing underneath it.
