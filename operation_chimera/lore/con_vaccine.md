# The Chimera Vaccine

The Chimeran virus, reframed as medicine. It is the same agent that made the hybrids - numbered variants, Chimera-9 the stable one - sold under a kinder name. The word you use depends on who is selling it. To the lab that built it, it was a weapon. To the people now dying who want it, it is a vaccine. To Osyra, it is the thing that was done to her.

## How it works

The virus does not add a costume on top of a person. It rewrites DNA at the cellular level and forces the body to rebuild around a new template. That is why most subjects died - the body could not survive the rewrite. The survivors are the ones whose systems re-stabilized on the new code.

The therapeutic logic falls straight out of that mechanism: if the agent can overwrite a whole genome, it can overwrite a genome that is broken. A rewrite engine does not care whether the code it replaces is "human" or "diseased." That is the door the cure walks through.

## What it could cure

Each of these shares one trait - the disease *is* the genome, so it cannot be fixed without rewriting it:

- **Terminal genetic disease** - the agent overwrites the faulty sequence. It does not overwrite *only* the faulty sequence. The patient lives - as a Chimeran.
- **Aggressive cancer** - the rewrite rebuilds clean tissue faster than the cancer corrupts it. A controlled burn, with the risk of trading one runaway cellular process for another.
- **A different strain already killing them** - the grimmest use. The only thing that can halt an unstable rewrite is a stable one (Chimera-9) layered on top. Not a cure. A death sentence overwritten with a life sentence.
- **Catastrophic trauma or organ failure** - the agent forces a rebuild a damaged body could not otherwise manage. Last-ditch, battlefield use.

## The cost

There is no free version:

- **You cannot choose "human but healthy."** The price of living is becoming Chimeran - red eyes, animal traits, a life of concealment.
- **The odds still kill.** Offering it as a cure means offering a coin-flip where heads is "monster" and tails is "corpse." Informed consent is nearly impossible, and historically the subjects were children who could not consent at all.
- **It cannot be taken back.** There is no reversal. The person who wakes up is not, in every sense, the person who went under.
- **It legitimizes the lab.** Every successful cure is an argument that the experiments were worth it.
- **Variant roulette.** Even if the rewrite holds, which traits emerge may not be controllable.

## Why it matters

The cure blurs the line between treatment and violation, because the lab already crossed it - they cured no one, they conquered children. The moment anyone uses the agent to genuinely save a life, they are standing exactly where the lab stood, with better intentions and the identical action. Osyra is both proofs at once: that it can work, and of what it costs.
