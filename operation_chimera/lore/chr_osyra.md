# Osyra Renaya

![Osyra Renaya](operation_chimera/lore/imgs/osyra0.jpg)

## Current
Osyra Renaya. A Chimeran Operator - part wolf, part human. Dark, brooding, and indifferent - she doesn't speak much until it's absolutely necessary. She saves people because it's her job, not because she wants to.

She hides her traits in civilian life using color contacts, hats, dresses - anything that works. She recently visited her biological grandmother Rosalyn for the first time. Vanity is her long-time record keeper at headquarters; Osyra debriefs her after every assignment. She is escorted everywhere by her bodyguard Korval.

## Physiology

A stable Chimera-9 expression - wolf traits on a human frame, the rewrite holding cleanly enough that she kept her mind.

**Enhancements**
- **Immune system:** robust. She shrugs off illness that levels the people around her (the lab's logs noted a "robust adaptive immune response").
- **Low-light vision:** superior - she reads near-dark like cloudy twilight, better than issued night-vision gear, which only gets in her way.
- **Hearing:** extended range and finely directional; her ears pivot to track sound.
- **Balance:** her tail is a real counterbalance - sharp directional changes, one-legged stability, fast recovery.
- **Build:** 6'4" and powerful, not subtle. A jump lands as a heavy, audible *thud*, never a silent drop.

**Costs**
- **Sensory overload:** the sharp hearing and smell cut both ways - loud noise and strong or foul odors hit harder and distract her.
- **Dog-toxic foods:** her wolf biology carries canine vulnerabilities. **Xylitol** is a genuine poison to her - fast, dangerous hypoglycemia. **Chocolate and caffeine** only upset her stomach: a running annoyance, not a threat.
- No special chemical frailty otherwise - tear gas hits her about like anyone else (it just lingers a little longer in sharper senses).

**Heat cycle** *(specific to Osyra so far)*
Her reproductive system runs the wolf program: a **seasonal estrous cycle, once a year in the fall**, cued by shortening daylight. It's predictable - she knows when it's coming and plans around it - and it conveniently lands on deer season, so her annual disappearance reads to everyone as "she likes to hunt."

It comes in stages: a restless, irritable lead-in (bad sleep, senses cranked, short temper, some early bleeding, her scent shifting enough that other Chimerans notice); a short, sharper window where the drive toward closeness spikes and her impulse control drops; then a hard crash, and back to baseline for another year. She manages it with hormonal regulators - a chemical "leash" she resents - or rides it out alone.

She is reproductively isolated: the rewrite made her a different species, so only another Chimera-9 could conceive with her, and a pregnancy would run human-length. A quiet, heavy fact she doesn't dwell on.

## Background

### Early Life
Osyra was given up for adoption as an infant; her parents' fate is unknown to her. Her aunt took her in and raised her until she passed away. After that, Osyra went into foster care. At sixteen, her foster guardian sold her to a medical facility.

### Experimentation
The facility conducted illegal human hybridization experiments using a virus engineered in multiple numbered variants. Osyra was injected with Chimera-9, the wolf-human variant. Other subjects received other numbered variants. Most subjects across all variants died or transformed into unstable forms. Osyra's body adapted - nervous system, musculature, and senses stabilized. She retained her intelligence but gained enhanced senses, physical enhancements, predatory instincts, and heightened environmental awareness. She emerged as a successful Chimera-9 subject.

### Escape
A mysterious external force attacked the facility before the lab could fully secure her. Explosions destroyed security systems and containment doors. She used the confusion to escape. She saw the lab owner alive and watching from an observation deck. The facility was officially listed as an industrial accident.

### After the Lab
She fled the wreckage into the woods and lived rough for a couple of months - wandering, stealing what she needed to survive, no one left who was supposed to keep her. Then a mentor found her, someone who had recognized her in the lab's surviving paperwork, and took her in, training her for a stretch. It was the first time since her aunt that someone invested in her instead of using her.

When the mentor judged her ready, they put word out that she was a strong recruitment candidate. Titan Reach Consulting reached out and, on the mentor's behalf, requested a video application - which opened into a clinical evaluation and Titan Reach's training program (boot camp). Roughly the path the lab might have steered her down itself, had it not been destroyed.

### Joining the Agency
She came out of training placed with the Agency, where she works now as an Operator - hunting down and fixing things, handling anomalous entities and situations. The pay was good. She got to be the secret they didn't point and laugh at.
