# Lore

## Current

### The Chimera Project
A lab created a virus that turns humans into Chimeran hybrids - part human, part animal. The virus was engineered in multiple numbered variants - Chimera-9 produced stable wolf-human hybrids (Osyra's variant); other numbered variants produced different animal traits or unstable transformations. The facility experimented on children. Most didn't survive. The lab was eventually destroyed when someone blew up part of the building. It was written off as a total loss.

The project did not begin as medicine. Its first purpose was **military** - to engineer human-animal hybrids as a battlefield asset, soldiers with predatory senses, instincts, and reflexes no normal human could match. Teenagers were chosen on purpose: young enough for the body to adapt to the rewrite, old enough to fight. The "cure" framing came later - a weapons program rebranded as medicine once the military use proved too unstable and too illegal. The healing pitch was the alibi, not the point.

### The Virus
The virus does not add a costume on top of a person - it rewrites DNA at the cellular level and forces the body to rebuild around a new template. That is why most subjects died: the body could not survive the rewrite. The survivors are the ones whose systems - nervous system, musculature, senses - re-stabilized on the new code.

Because it can overwrite a whole genome, the same agent can in principle overwrite a genome that is broken - which is what lets it be reframed as a cure. See [The Chimera Vaccine](operation_chimera/lore/con_vaccine.md).

### Chimerans
Humans altered by the Chimeran virus. The virus always gives red eyes. The specific animal traits depend on which numbered variant was administered - Chimera-9 produces wolf traits, other variants produce others. Any enhanced abilities depend on those traits. Their appearance causes problems in civilian life, so they conceal themselves using contacts, hats, and clothing.

Surviving the body is not the same as keeping the mind. Chimera-9 is the most stable variant with the highest survival rate, but most who live through the rewrite lose their humanity, sliding into feral, predatory forms - alive as animals, not as people. Osyra is the rare exception who survived *and* kept her self. Some of the things the Agency sends her to hunt are feral Chimera-9 survivors: the version of her that the dice didn't pick.

### The Agency
A chain-of-command organization that deals with anomalous entities and situations. Five ranks, lowest to highest:

- **Janitor** - separate teams that show up after Headquarters decides the Operators are done. They clean up what's left at the site. When Janitors arrive, the work is over.
- **Rookie** - Operator in training. Field-track but not yet trusted with their own case.
- **Operator** - full field rank. Hunts down anomalies, retrieves artifacts, handles situations. Osyra is an Operator.
- **Handler** - manages Operators from headquarters. Runs the desk, keeps the records, makes the calls. Each Operator has a Handler; some Handlers run more than one Operator. Vanity is a Handler - nicknamed Wiki because she keeps the record on everything.
- **Director** - top of the agency.

Operators are assigned bodyguards in the field (Korval is Osyra's). Codenames are used during formal events - mission radio chatter, official briefings, filed reports - and dropped for real names otherwise.

### Anomalous Entities
Otherworldly creatures, objects that warp reality, things that bend the mind. They don't follow normal rules. Some ignore Chimerans entirely. Some respond to them specifically.

### The World
A modern setting. Cities with metropolis centers that give way to rural neighborhoods. Hotels, cars, Polaroid cameras. A world that looks normal on the surface but has things hiding underneath it.
