# Titan Reach - the through-line antagonist

Source: author (2026-07-23). Titan Reach is far more than the Borne Weapon recruiter - they are the standing antagonist behind the whole setup.

## What they are
- **Funded the lab** that experimented on Osyra (and the other Chimeran kids).
- **Recruited and trained her** - excitedly. The video application (Borne Weapon ch1), clinical eval, boot camp are all Titan Reach.
- Their goal: **make super-soldiers.** It "kinda worked" - Osyra is a success case.

## It's a real corporation - most staff don't know
- Titan Reach is a **legitimate corporation**, not a cartoon-villain org. **Most employees are unaware** of the Chimeran program / experiments.
- The inhumane super-soldier work is **compartmentalized** - a hidden part of the company, known to a small circle. Ordinary staff are just doing normal corporate jobs.
- Proofreading: never portray "Titan Reach" as uniformly complicit or all-knowing. When a Titan Reach character acts, distinguish whether they're in the compartment or an oblivious employee. The antagonist is the program and the people running it, not every badge.

## Ongoing threat (present day)
- They **surveil the Agency** Osyra works for. The Agency is watched by Titan Reach.
- They **stalk any Chimeran** to continue research. Chimerans are ongoing research subjects to them.
- Later arc: when Osyra posts about them, **Titan Reach is the "they"** that tries to declare her non-human (to reclaim her as property/research, defeat the personhood claim). She wins; see `planned_arc.md`.

## Proofreading implications
- Titan Reach appearing "just" as a past recruiter undersells them - they should read as a present, watching threat.
- The Agency and Titan Reach are DISTINCT: Agency = her employer; Titan Reach = funder/creator that watches the Agency. Don't conflate.
- Any Chimeran being observed/followed can plausibly be Titan Reach's doing.
