## Overall shape (author, 2026-07-24)

# PART 1 - The Making (origin -> boot camp graduation, ages 0-20)

Emotional throughline: a person made into an asset by everyone who was supposed to keep her, who nonetheless keeps her self - and, right at the end, chooses the collar herself because it is the first thing anyone ever offered as a *choice*. Part 1 is why the ownership fight in Part 3 cuts so deep: she has been "owned" her whole life; the law only ever made it official at the end.

Blood Relation (present-day, age 29) is NOT Part 1 - it belongs later as the family frame. Part 1 is chronological early life. The written *Borne Weapon* chapters are the boot-camp spine (the back third of Part 1).

## Act 1a - Nothing (ages 0-16, the pre-lab decade)
- **The kept-on-nothing years** - poverty that doesn't bend; parents who won't be seen. Grandmother Rosalyn exists in the phone book, never called. (Establish the absence that Part 3's family arc pays off.)
- **The school notices** - a nurse, a form, a child too thin and too quiet. The first adult to really see her - and what "being seen" costs her.
- **The state takes her; nobody looks for family** - processed, not rescued. Rosalyn shares the surname and is never contacted. (The system's first failure to treat her as a person - rhymes with Titan Reach later.)
- **The foster decade** - a few homes, each colder than the last. Ten years of being handled. No photographs exist of her after age 6 (pays off Rosalyn's Polaroids at 29).

## Act 1b - Sold (age 16, the lab)
- **The guardian sells her** - the coldest one, who saw her as an asset from the start. Schooling ends. (The literal first sale of her body - the thing Titan Reach later dresses up in legal language.)
- **The injection** - Chimera-9. The others get other variants. Clinical, not cruel-for-cruelty's-sake; that's what makes it worse.
- **Most die; she stabilizes and keeps her mind** - the rare exception. What it is to wake up rewritten and still be yourself. The wolf traits arrive here - not as horror, as *her* now.
- **The facility burns** - a mysterious external force, explosions, containment fails. She escapes in the confusion. (Keep the attacker a mystery per canon.)
- **The owner on the observation deck** - she sees him watching, alive, as she runs. Unresolved thread planted for later. Written off as an industrial accident.

## Act 1c - The Fray (ages 16-18, rough living)
- **Into the woods** - alone, rewritten, free and with nothing. The body works; the world doesn't want it.
- **The long drift** - two years homeless through towns and streets, stealing and scavenging. Learning to hide the ears, the eyes - the disguise starts as survival, not mission.
- **Low point** - the version of her closest to feral without crossing it; what keeps her human when nothing is holding her to it.

## Act 1d - Uta (age ~18, turned back into a person)
- **The dance club** - she wanders in; Uta is bartending (before Aurora Pulse). The offered share of weed - the first decency with no angle in years.
- **Smoking friends** - easy, repeated, uncomplicated contact. She relearns being around people.
- **Uta takes her in** - the first person ever to invest in her rather than use her. Shelter, stability, how to *pass*, how to want a future. (No combat training - that's later.) This is the emotional heart of Part 1; the relationship that proves she can be a person is the thing the program then recruits.

## Act 1e - Borne Weapon: the recruitment + boot camp (ages 18-20) - LARGELY WRITTEN
Threads the existing `stories/borne_weapon/` chapters. The irony: once Uta makes her a person, Titan Reach makes her a weapon again - but this time she says yes.
- **01 The Application** (written) - the smoking-circle referral; the video application. Titan Reach's excited recruitment. She volunteers.
- **02 The Verification / 03 The Clinical** (written) - Vasquez, Chen, Dr. Ellen; hormonal regulators prescribed. The clinical eval - being measured again, but as a candidate, not a subject. The unsettling echo of the lab, chosen this time.
- **05 The Offer** (written) - what they're really offering; why she takes it (the pay was good; the belonging; nowhere else to be a weapon safely).
- **06 The Combine / 07 The Prototype** (written) - training proper; equipment/first real test of the built body.
- **08 Shark Attack / 09 Broken Down / 10 The Trials** (written) - the hardest passages of boot camp; broken down and rebuilt; the final trials.
- **Graduation - the forest, age 20** (where *Borne Weapon* ends) - Part 1 closes. She is an operator now. End on the double edge: the most she has ever belonged to anything, and she got there by being made a weapon a second time - by choice. Sets up Part 2 (the Agency placement, "the pay was good").
