VTuber Creation Guide

A Step-by-Step Tutorial for Creating and Animating Your Virtual Avatar

Archived Tutorial Document

Introduction

This guide provides a structured approach to creating and animating a VTuber avatar, even on low-end devices. Whether you're a beginner or looking for optimized tools, this document will help you design, customize, and bring your virtual persona to life.

What is a VTuber?

A VTuber (Virtual YouTuber) is a content creator who uses a computer-generated avatar, typically anime-styled, that is animated using motion capture or tracking software. The avatar moves and reacts in real-time based on the creator's movements and voice.

Section 1: Default VTuber Creation Method

Step 1: Prepare a Reference Image

Before designing your avatar, gather visual references to ensure accuracy in colors, proportions, and style.

Where to Find References

  • Pixiv - A popular platform for anime-style artwork
  • DeviantArt - Features a wide variety of character designs
  • Game or anime screenshots - Useful for replicating existing characters

Why Use a Reference?

  • Helps maintain consistency in design
  • Ensures proper color matching
  • Guides facial and clothing details

Step 2: Create Your Avatar in VRoid Studio

VRoid Studio is a free 3D character creator designed for VTuber models.

Download & Installation:

Official Site: VRoid Studio

Steam Version: Available on Steam for easy updates

Key Features

  • Intuitive sliders for facial and body customization
  • Hair, clothing, and accessory editors
  • Supports importing additional assets

Additional Resources

Booth.pm - Marketplace for free and paid VRoid assets (e.g., hairstyles, outfits)

Exporting Your Model

  1. Save as .vroid (editable project file)
  2. Export as .vrm (for use in motion capture software)

Tip: Always keep a backup of your .vroid file so you can make edits later. The .vrm export is optimized for use in tracking software but cannot be easily edited.

Section 2: VTuber Creation for Low-End Devices

If your computer struggles with 3D modeling or real-time tracking, these alternatives provide lightweight solutions.

Animation Tool: Veadotube Mini

For creators who want minimal resource usage or prefer a simpler 2D aesthetic, Veadotube Mini offers an excellent alternative to full 3D VTubing.

Attribute Details
Website Veadotube Mini
Best For 2D PNGTuber-style avatars
Features Minimal CPU/GPU usage, supports face tracking via webcam

What is a PNGTuber?

A PNGTuber uses static or minimally animated 2D images that react to voice input, typically switching between "talking" and "idle" states. This approach requires far fewer system resources than full 3D avatar tracking.

Section 3: Workflow Summary

Quick Reference: Creation Paths

Path Avatar Type Tools Needed Hardware Requirements
Standard 3D Full 3D VTuber VRoid Studio + VSeeFace Webcam, moderate PC specs
3D No-Webcam Full 3D VTuber VRoid Studio + VMagicMirror No webcam needed, moderate PC specs
Low-End 2D PNGTuber Krita/GIMP + Veadotube Mini Minimal specs, webcam optional

Step-by-Step Workflow

  1. Design Phase: Use VRoid Studio with reference images to create your 3D model, or create 2D artwork in Krita/GIMP for a PNGTuber
  2. Export Phase: Save your work in editable format (.vroid or .psd/.kra) and export for use (.vrm or .png)
  3. Animation Phase: Import your avatar into your chosen tracking software:
    • VSeeFace for webcam-based 3D tracking
    • VMagicMirror for keyboard/mouse-controlled 3D animation
    • Veadotube Mini for 2D PNGTuber animation
  4. Streaming Setup: Configure OBS or your streaming software to capture your avatar with a transparent background

Conclusion & Next Steps

Creating a VTuber avatar is more accessible than ever, with free tools available for every skill level and hardware configuration. Here's a quick recap:

Summary:

Asset Creation: Utilize Clip Studio, Krita, or GIMP for custom edits
