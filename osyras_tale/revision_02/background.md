I'm nervous. There's so many things that can go wrong.

I say this while looking out the window as my chauffeur drives me to my destination. Up front, behind the wheel, is Korval—a tough, Russian ex-military guy. He's primarily my chauffeur and bodyguard. He always follows me everywhere. Not that I need it. I'm usually the dangerous one.

I recently got word that the agency found one of my biological family members and gave me the opportunity to visit. I took it. My grandmother: Rosalyn Renaya. Despite my protests—I don't need a bodyguard just to blend in as family—my boss insisted and would not back down.

"How much further until we get there?" I wondered out loud.

"About thirty minutes," Korval answered in his thick Russian accent. Then he added, "Why are you so impatient? You're always calm."

A strange sensation washed over me. I'd never wanted to be close to family before. Maybe this was a new experience. I had nothing to say back. No reply. I stared out the window as the metropolis turned into rural neighborhoods.

"Okay, we're just about there," Korval said. "Make sure you dress appropriately."

For a moment, I'd nearly forgotten that my grandmother doesn't know about my altered form.

---

I am Chimera now. I wasn't always Chimera. This came from a lab. My true parents died when I was young. I was given to a foster parent who immediately sold me to a medical facility. They experimented on me. They experimented on many other kids. Most of them didn't make it. I did.

I became a Chimera—part wolf, part human. You couldn't classify me as human or animal. While they were running their tests, there was a breach. Someone blew up part of the building. The facilities shut down, and I got free. They wrote it off as a total loss.

But then came the problem: how to get work? How to get money when I look like this? I was discovered by someone sifting through the paperwork in the building. They recognized me, found me, and offered me a job. Provide my capabilities—which have proven adequate. They wanted me to hunt down and fix things. Deal with anomalous entities and situations. The pay was good. I got to be the secret they didn't pointed and laugh at.

I wanted to go outside occasionally, so I learned how to conceal my form:

· Color contacts to hide my red eyes
· Big hats to hide my ears
· Long dresses to hide my tail

Didn't need to smile much. You couldn't see my canines anyway.

People I come into contact with just think I'm angry. That's good enough. They leave me alone.

After a while lost in thought, I was suddenly snapped back to reality. Korval said we had about fifteen minutes until we arrived. This was my sign to get ready—prepare any way I saw fit. Double-check my dress was set just right. Hat hiding my ears. No smiling. Standard procedure.

Today was a relatively warm day. The sun was shining, and I looked like a bronze beauty.
